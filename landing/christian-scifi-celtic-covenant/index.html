The Covenant That Travelled West

When Nebuchadnezzar's armies entered Jerusalem, not everything was taken. The prophet Jeremiah received a commission: carry the covenant promise westward. Tea Tephi — Zedekiah's daughter, heir of the Davidic line — carried the right. Together, they carried what God had promised would not be allowed to die: the unbroken thread of the throne that Revelation promises will be claimed by its rightful King.

Ezekiel named it plainly: an overturn, an overturn, an overturn. Three prophetic movements of the sceptre across history, each one documented in the Celtic oral tradition, the genealogical records of Ireland's high kings, and the annals that the monks of Iona preserved. The Stone and the Sceptre Chronicles is the narrative of those three overturns — told through the eyes of those who carried the covenant without fully understanding what they were carrying.

"An overturn, an overturn, an overturn, will I make it: and it shall be no more, until he come whose right it is; and I will give it him."

— Ezekiel 21:27

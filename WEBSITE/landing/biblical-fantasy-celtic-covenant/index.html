Where the Legends Were Keeping Something Real

The Celtic oral tradition preserved genealogies, migration routes, and foundational stories that mainstream history dismissed as legend. The Stone and the Sceptre Chronicles reads those legends as what the communities who preserved them always said they were: the outer memory of a real event, a real bloodline, and a real covenant that God carried west from Jerusalem when He refused to let Babylon have the last word.

Tea Tephi — daughter of Zedekiah, carrier of the Davidic right — is attested in Irish genealogical records as Tamar Tephi. The Stone of Destiny is attested in Scottish and Irish tradition as the Lia Fáil. Jeremiah's presence in Ireland is attested in a tradition preserved at Tara. The Stone and the Sceptre Chronicles follows this thread with narrative care and historical respect — tracing what the legends were keeping and why the covenant required a Celtic home to hold it.

## Stage 1: SHIDDUKHIN — The Match and Negotiation

### The Ancient Practice

In biblical times, marriages were arranged by fathers to ensure stability and benefit for both families.

**Key Elements:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **The Negotiation** | Fathers (or representatives) arranged the match |
| **The Friend of the Bridegroom** | A trusted intermediary who handled negotiations |
| **The Mohar (Bride Price)** | Payment from groom's family to bride's family |
| **The Mattan** | Gifts from groom directly to the bride |

### Christ's Fulfillment

**The Eternal Negotiation:**

The "match" between Christ and His Bride was arranged in eternity past:

> *"All that the Father gives Me will come to Me"* — John 6:37

> *"I have manifested Your name to the men whom You gave Me out of the world; they were Yours and You gave them to Me"* — John 17:6

> *"He chose us in Him before the foundation of the world"* — Ephesians 1:4

The Father "gave" the Bride to the Son before creation. The match was made in the eternal counsel of the Godhead.

**The Friend of the Bridegroom:**

John the Baptist explicitly identified himself in this role:

> *"He who has the bride is the bridegroom; but the friend of the bridegroom, who stands and hears him, rejoices greatly because of the bridegroom's voice. So this joy of mine has been made full."* — John 3:29

John's role was to:
- Prepare the way for the Groom
- Introduce the Bride to the Groom
- Step aside once the Groom arrived

**The Bride Price (Mohar):**

What was the payment that secured the Bride?

> *"You were not redeemed with perishable things like silver or gold from your futile way of life inherited from your forefathers, but with precious blood, as of a lamb unblemished and spotless, the blood of Christ."* — 1 Peter 1:18-19

> *"For you have been bought with a price"* — 1 Corinthians 6:20

The Bride Price was the **blood of Christ** — the most precious payment in the universe. No higher price could be paid. The Father received infinite satisfaction; the Bride was secured forever.

**The Mattan (Gifts to the Bride):**

What gifts did the Groom give directly to His Bride?

> *"In Him you also, after listening to the message of truth, the gospel of your salvation—having also believed, you were sealed in Him with the Holy Spirit of promise, who is given as a pledge of our inheritance"* — Ephesians 1:13-14

The **Holy Spirit** is the Groom's gift to the Bride — the "pledge" (arrabon, a down payment or engagement ring) guaranteeing the inheritance to come.

## Stage 2: ERUSIN — The Betrothal

### The Ancient Practice

The Erusin was the **legally binding** stage. Unlike modern engagement, this was a formal covenant — the couple was considered married in the eyes of the law, though they did not yet live together or consummate the union.

**Key Elements:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **The Ketubah** | A written contract outlining the groom's obligations |
| **The Cup of Wine** | Shared to seal the covenant |
| **The Period of Separation** | Groom returns to father's house to prepare the bridal chamber; bride stays with her family preparing herself |

### Christ's Fulfillment

**The Betrothal Ceremony: The Last Supper**

> *"And when He had taken a cup and given thanks, He gave it to them, saying, 'Drink from it, all of you; for this is My blood of the covenant, which is poured out for many for forgiveness of sins.'"* — Matthew 26:27-28

> *"This cup is the new covenant in My blood"* — Luke 22:20

At the Last Supper, Christ:
1. Established the **New Covenant** (the Ketubah — the marriage contract)
2. Shared the **Cup of Wine** with His Bride (sealing the covenant)
3. Outlined His obligations: forgiveness, eternal life, presence, inheritance

The disciples were representatives of the Bride, entering into covenant with the Groom.

**The Bride Price Paid: The Cross**

The next day, Christ paid the Mohar in full:

> *"It is finished"* — John 19:30 (Tetelestai — "Paid in full")

The Cross was the payment of the Bride Price. The covenant was now sealed in blood — irrevocable, eternal.

**The Period of Separation: The Church Age**

After the betrothal, the groom would return to his father's house to build a "bridal chamber" (an addition to the family home). He would return for his bride only when the father declared the home ready.

> *"In My Father's house are many dwelling places; if it were not so, I would have told you; for I go to prepare a place for you. If I go and prepare a place for you, I will come again and receive you to Myself, that where I am, there you may be also."* — John 14:2-3

Christ ascended to His Father's house. He is **preparing a place** for His Bride. We are currently in the **Period of Separation** — the betrothal has happened, but the wedding has not yet occurred.

**The Bride's Preparation:**

During this separation, the bride was expected to:
- Remain faithful to her betrothed
- Prepare her garments
- Keep herself "set apart" (sanctified) for her husband
- Wait for his return

> *"Let us rejoice and be glad and give the glory to Him, for the marriage of the Lamb has come and His bride has made herself ready. It was given to her to clothe herself in fine linen, bright and clean; for the fine linen is the righteous acts of the saints."* — Revelation 19:7-8

The Church's task during this age:
1. **Remain faithful** — not committing spiritual adultery with the world
2. **Prepare garments** — the "righteous acts of the saints"
3. **Stay set apart** — sanctification
4. **Wait** — "Maranatha, come Lord Jesus"

## Stage 3: NISSUIN — The Marriage Ceremony and Feast

### The Ancient Practice

The final stage was the "taking" (nissuin means "to lift up" or "to carry") of the bride. This was often a surprise.

**Key Elements:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **The Procession** | The groom leads a torchlight procession to the bride's house |
| **The Shout** | Often announced by a shout or shofar (trumpet) blast |
| **The Huppah** | The couple stands under a canopy, symbolizing their new household |
| **The Seven-Day Feast** | A massive celebration lasting seven days |
| **Consummation** | The marriage consummated on the first night |

### Christ's Fulfillment (Future)

**The Coming of the Groom:**

> *"But of that day and hour no one knows, not even the angels of heaven, nor the Son, but the Father alone."* — Matthew 24:36

Just as the ancient groom came when **the father** declared the home ready, so Christ will return when the Father determines. The Groom Himself does not know the hour — only the Father.

**The Shout and Trumpet:**

> *"For the Lord Himself will descend from heaven with a shout, with the voice of the archangel and with the trumpet of God, and the dead in Christ will rise first. Then we who are alive and remain will be caught up together with them in the clouds to meet the Lord in the air, and so we shall always be with the Lord."* — 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17

The ancient wedding procession was announced with a shout and trumpet. Christ's return will be announced with:
- A **shout** (the command)
- The voice of the **archangel** (Michael — the Bride's guardian)
- The **trumpet of God**

The Bride is "caught up" (harpazo — raptured) to meet her Groom.

**The Wedding Supper:**

> *"Blessed are those who are invited to the marriage supper of the Lamb"* — Revelation 19:9

The seven-day feast finds its fulfillment in the **Marriage Supper of the Lamb** — the great celebration in heaven.

**The Consummation:**

> *"And I saw the holy city, new Jerusalem, coming down out of heaven from God, made ready as a bride adorned for her husband."* — Revelation 21:2

The ultimate consummation is the **eternal union** — God dwelling with His people forever. The New Jerusalem *is* the Bride, the dwelling place where Groom and Bride are never again separated.

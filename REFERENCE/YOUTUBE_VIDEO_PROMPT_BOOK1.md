## MASTER BRIEF

> Produce a cinematic book trailer for **THE NEPHILIM CHRONICLES: Book 1 — THE CYDONIAN OATHS** by Kerman Gild. This is not a soft, literary trailer. This is a war dispatch. A recruiting poster for the end of days. The tone is epic, ancient, and absolutely certain of itself. No whimsy. No irony. The supernatural is real, it is coming for the world, and one exhausted Irish warrior is all that stands between the present and the Tribulation.
>
> The video should feel like the opening cinematic of the most ambitious game never made — if that game was written by Tom Clancy, supervised by the angel Raphael, and scored by Hans Zimmer on the worst night of his life.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **The sword must never look magical.** It looks like old metal until it resonates. The acoustic visualization is wave physics, not fantasy glow. Ripples, standing waves, refracted light — not fire or magic sparks.
- **Lord Vârcolac must never look like a movie vampire.** No capes. No fangs on display. He is five thousand years old and he has learned patience. The horror is in the stillness.
- **Raphael must barely be visible.** A figure of light at the edge of perception. If the audience can fully see him, it's wrong. He is not a character yet in Book 1 — he is a presence.
- **Antarctica must feel like an ending beginning.** Cold, enormous, ancient. The glow from below is not horror-movie red — it is warm amber. Something vast is warm down there. That is worse.
- **Cian is never melodramatic.** He does not pose. He does not brood at the camera. He is tactical, exhausted, precise — and when he looks dangerous, it is because he IS dangerous. 2,636 years of kill count.

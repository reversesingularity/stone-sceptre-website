## PROJECT OVERVIEW

**The Nephilim Chronicles** is a five-book biblical fantasy/apocalyptic thriller series.

**Protagonist:** Cian mac Morna — a Celtic warrior from 586 BCE kept alive by Methuselah's Sword for 2,600+ years. He is the **Guardian of the Two Witnesses** (Enoch and Elijah) prophesied in Revelation 11.

**Thematic Verse:** John 15:13 — "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends."

**Core Premise:** The end times are the *completion* of what began before the Flood — same enemies (Nephilim spirits, Naamah, Watcher influence), same protagonists (Enoch returns), same pattern (corruption → judgment → remnant saved).

## RECENT CANON UPDATES (JANUARY 19-20, 2026)

### Article XI: The Four Horsemen + Hades Framework

**Key Insight:** There are FIVE figures, not four.

| Figure | Role | Action |
|--------|------|--------|
| Conquest | First Horseman | Rides white horse |
| War | Second Horseman | Rides red horse |
| Famine | Third Horseman | Rides black horse |
| **Thanatos** | Fourth Horseman (Death) | Rides pale horse |
| **Hades** | The Follower | **Does NOT ride** — walks behind, gathers the dead |

**Critical Canon:**
- Horsemen are aligned with **HEAVEN**, not Hell — they serve the Lamb
- They **OPPOSE** the Unholy Trinity — disrupt the Beast's controlled apocalypse
- Hades is a "waiting room," not an enemy — receives Cian at death, releases him at resurrection
- Their stoicism stems from **existential acceptance** — they know they'll be cast into Lake of Fire when sin is eradicated (Rev 20:14)
- 1 Cor 15:55-56: "O death (thanatos), where is thy sting? O grave (hades), where is thy victory?"

CHAPTER 5 (DRAFT) SCENE: THE CELESTIAL BIOLOGY LESSON

Setting: Cian’s workshop (safehouse basement, Austin)

Cian stood at his workbench, surrounded by the controlled chaos of two millennia of weapons development. Sample vials lined one shelf. Metallurgical equipment hummed on another. Three monitors displayed molecular structures, chemical formulas, and a rotating 3D model of something that looked like crystallized hatred.

Mo Chrá leaned against the wall in its current form — a bastard sword, the compromise it had grudgingly accepted for indoor work. It was doing that thing it did when it was pretending not to listen.

“Liaigh,” Cian said without looking up. “I need help with something.”

“THIS UTTERANCE HATH HERALDED CATASTROPHE ON NO FEWER THAN SEVEN HUNDRED OCCASIONS ACROSS THE SPAN OF THY MORTAL TENURE.”

“I’m trying to figure out how to containerize fluoroantimonic acid. Would industrial diamonds work? Synthetic corundum? I’m thinking if I could—”

“NO.”

The word hit like a physical force. Cian actually flinched.

“IT SHALL NOT HOLD. THE PERIL THOU DOST CONTEMPLATE EXCEEDETH THY COMPREHENSION BY ORDERS OF MAGNITUDE.”

“It’s just chemistry, Liaigh. Harder container, stronger acid—”

“ATTEND, CHILD OF MORNA, FOR THOU DOST REASON FROM FALSE PREMISES.”

Cian sighed and put down his stylus. When Liaigh said attend, the lecture was coming whether he wanted it or not.

“GEMSTONES — WHETHER WROUGHT BY TERRESTRIAL PROCESSES OR ENGINEERED BY MORTAL ARTIFICE — ARE CONSTITUTED OF MINERALS, AND MINERALS ARE CONSTITUTED OF ATOMS ARRAYED IN CRYSTALLINE LATTICES OF GEOMETRIC PRECISION. THE SUPERACID THOU DOST CONTEMPLATE ATTACKETH NOT BY MECHANICAL FORCE BUT BY CHEMICAL COMPOSITION. HARDNESS AVAILETH THEE NOTHING.”

Liaigh's voice shifted into what Cian privately called "the lecture register" — still empyreal, but with the patient cadence of a teacher who had explained thermodynamics to prophets.

“THE SILICATES — QUARTZ, AMETHYST, CITRINE, BERYL, EMERALD, AQUAMARINE, TOPAZ — THESE ARE EDIFICES BUILT UPON SILICON-OXYGEN BONDS. FLUOROANTIMONIC ACID DISSOLVETH SUCH BONDS WITH THE SAME ENTHUSIASM THAT FIRE CONSUMETH DRY TIMBER. THE OXIDES — CORUNDUM, FROM WHICH THY SAPPHIRES AND RUBIES DERIVE, AND SPINEL OF MAGNESIUM AND ALUMINUM — THESE ARE METAL OXIDES, AND THE SUPERACID STRIPPETH OXYGEN FROM METAL AS A WOLF STRIPPETH FLESH FROM BONE, LEAVING NAUGHT BUT FLUORIDE SALTS IN ITS WAKE.”

“What about—”

“THE CARBONATES — CALCITE, ARAGONITE, MALACHITE — THESE REACT WITH VIOLENT EFFERVESCENCE UPON CONTACT WITH STRONG ACIDS OF ANY VARIETY. ORGANIC GEMSTONES — AMBER, JET, PEARL — THESE BEING CARBON-BASED, WOULD BE CONSUMED WITH EVEN GREATER ALACRITY.”

“Diamond,” Cian said. “Diamond is—”

“DIAMOND IS THE SOLE SPECIMEN MERITING CONSIDERATION, AND EVEN THIS PROVETH INSUFFICIENT. WHILST DIAMOND RESISTETH MANY ACIDS BY VIRTUE OF ITS CARBON-CARBON BONDS, FLUOROANTIMONIC ACID IS NOT ‘MANY ACIDS.’ IT IS A SUPERACID THAT PROTONATES SUBSTANCES WHICH OTHER ACIDS CANNOT TOUCH. DIAMOND CAN BE ATTACKED BY STRONG FLUORINATING AGENTS, AND THY PROPOSED CONTAINMENT MEDIUM RELEASETH HYDROGEN FLUORIDE AND OTHER REACTIVE SPECIES AS A MATTER OF COURSE.”

The angel paused — a rhetorical pause, Cian knew, for emphasis.

“THE RESULT: DIAMOND PROVIDETH NO GUARANTEE OF STABILITY, AND OVER TEMPORAL DURATION WOULD CERTAINLY FAIL. THOU WOULDST CREATE NOT A WEAPON BUT A SUICIDE DEVICE OF EXQUISITE IRONY.”

“Alright, alright.” Cian raised both hands in surrender. “Enough with the chemistry lesson. I know only PTFE works for containment, but the dispersal would be too slow. The regeneration outpaces the delivery mechanism.”

He turned back to his monitors, pulling up a new file.

“I’m giving up on fluoroantimonic. Let me think…”

He scrolled through formulas, calculations, field notes from three centuries of anti-regeneration research.
“How about this — piranha solution? Sulfuric acid and hydrogen peroxide. They use it in semiconductor fabrication for dissolving organic contamination from reactor components. Violently exothermic reaction with tissue, produces heat and gas expansion simultaneously, and it’s stable in borosilicate glass containers until mixed.”

“THE THERMAL BLOOM WOULD INDEED PROVE DISRUPTIVE TO REGENERATIVE PROCESSES. HOWEVER—”

“However?”

“THE OXIDATION WOULD BE INDISCRIMINATE. THOU WOULDST REQUIRE DELIVERY DIRECTLY TO THE CORE MASS, ELSE THE TARGET MIGHT SHED THE AFFECTED TISSUE FASTER THAN THE REACTION PROPAGATES.”

“Noted.” Cian made a notation. “What about a dual-payload approach? Fifty-cal thermite rounds — micro-charge in the core that ignites on impact. Four thousand degrees Fahrenheit burning inside the wound channel.”

“THERMITE REQUIRETH OXYGEN. THE INTERIOR OF A NEPHILIM’S REGENERATIVE MATRIX IS NOTABLY ANAEROBIC ONCE SEALED.”

“Thermate, then. Self-oxidizing mixture. Or…” He was warming up now, mind racing. “Cryogenic capsule. Flash-freeze the tissue before regeneration can initiate. Liquid nitrogen core, ceramic shell that shatters on impact—”

“THE NEPHILIM REGENERATIVE RESPONSE OPERATETH AT TEMPERATURES THAT WOULD VITRIFY MORTAL FLESH. SUPERCOOLING WOULD DELAY BUT NOT PREVENT RECONSTITUTION.”

“Dual-phase payload. Rapid expansion and contraction in sequence — create micro-fractures in the cellular structure before regeneration can establish coherent tissue—”

“APPROACHING PLAUSIBILITY.”

Cian paused. That was practically a compliment.

“Or this.” He pulled up another schematic. “Expanding gas projectile. Capsule containing compressed inert gas — helium or argon — that violently expands on impact. Internal rupture, pressure shock, organ displacement—”

“ARGON WOULD PROVE MORE EFFECTIVE. DENSER THAN HELIUM, SUPERIOR TISSUE PENETRATION DURING EXPANSION PHASE.”

“Or pressurized biofoam. Expanding polymer matrix that fills the wound channel and hardens, mechanically preventing tissue reconstruction—”

“THE NEPHILIM REGENERATIVE RESPONSE WOULD SIMPLY INCORPORATE THE FOREIGN MATERIAL INTO ITS RECONSTITUTED MASS. THOU WOULDST SUCCEED ONLY IN ARMORING THINE ENEMY FROM THE INSIDE.”

“Damn.” Cian crossed it off. “Okay — moisture-activated compound. Calcium oxide, maybe. Releases heat when exposed to blood plasma, causes localized burns throughout the wound channel—”

“CALCIUM OXIDE — QUICKITE — HATH BEEN EMPLOYED AGAINST NEPHILIM SINCE THE TIME OF THY FOREBEARS IN IRELAND. THEIR REGENERATIVE RESPONSE HATH… ADAPTED.”

Cian slammed his hand on the desk.

“That’s the problem! Every time I develop something new, they adapt. Eight centuries ago, Bás Dearg worked perfectly — one hit, dissolution from the inside out. Now I’m seeing recovery rates that should be impossible. They’re evolving, Liaigh. Learning. Getting better at surviving what I throw at them.”

“THOU ART CORRECT.”

“That’s not comforting.”

“IT WAS NOT INTENDED AS COMFORT. IT WAS INTENDED AS CONFIRMATION THAT THY OBSERVATIONS POSSESS ACCURACY.”

From the corner, Mo Chrá made a sound. Not quite humming. More like… commentary.

Cian glanced at the sword.

“Something to add?”

The sword’s presence shifted — that particular vibration that meant it was thinking about communicating.

"AH," Liaigh said, with what Cian could swear was amusement. "THE BLADE HATH OPINIONS."

“The blade always has opinions. It just rarely shares them.”

“PERHAPS BECAUSE ITS WIELDER RARELY LISTENETH.”

Mo Chrá hummed again — louder this time.

“THE PATRIARCH’S BLADE OBSERVETH,” Liaigh translated, his tone shifting to something almost… conversational, “THAT THOU DOST FOCUS UPON CHEMICAL DESTRUCTION WHEN THE NEPHILIM REGENERATIVE RESPONSE IS FUNDAMENTALLY SPIRITUAL IN NATURE.”

“I can’t fight spiritual problems with spiritual weapons. The sword kills them permanently, but I can’t be everywhere at once. I need ranged options.”

“THE BLADE SUGGESTETH THAT PERHAPS THE ISSUE IS NOT THE PAYLOAD BUT THE TARGET SELECTION.”

“Meaning?”

Mo Chrá’s hum took on a distinctly smug quality.

“THE BLADE NOTETH THAT NEPHILIM REGENERATION REQUIRETH A COHERENT SPIRITUAL ANCHOR — WHAT MORTALS MIGHT TERM A ‘SOUL FRAGMENT’ INHERITED FROM THE WATCHER PROGENITOR. DISRUPT THE ANCHOR, AND CHEMICAL DESTRUCTION BECOMETH MERELY… INCONVENIENT RATHER THAN INEFFECTIVE.”

Cian stared at the sword.

“You’ve known this for how long?”

The sword’s presence radiated something that felt distinctly like a shrug.

“THOU DIDST NOT INQUIRE,” Liaigh supplied, his voice carrying the particular weight of someone who had watched this dynamic play out for twenty-six centuries. “THE BLADE POSSESSETH INFORMATION APLENTY. IT SIMPLY DECLINETH TO VOLUNTEER THAT WHICH IS NOT DIRECTLY SOLICITED.”

“Petty. That’s what that is. Four thousand years old and petty.”

Mo Chrá’s hum contained absolutely no denial whatsoever.

“THE PATRIARCH’S BLADE,” Liaigh continued, and Cian could hear the eye-roll in his voice despite the empyreal register, “FURTHER SUGGESTETH THAT IF THOU DOST TRULY WISH TO DISRUPT NEPHILIM REGENERATION WITH RANGED WEAPONS, THOU SHOULDST CONSIDER INCORPORATING MATERIALS THAT INTERFERE WITH SPIRITUAL COHERENCE. CERTAIN ACOUSTIC FREQUENCIES. SPECIFIC CRYSTALLINE STRUCTURES. ITEMS THAT HAVE BEEN BLESSED, CONSECRATED, OR EXPOSED TO SUSTAINED PRAYER.”

“Blessed thermite rounds.”

“IN ESSENCE.”

Cian rubbed his temples.

“You two rehearse this, don’t you? The withholding of information until I’ve exhausted myself, then the perfectly timed revelation?”

"I CANNOT SPEAK FOR THE BLADE," Liaigh said, "BUT I ASSURE THEE THAT MINE OWN PEDAGOGICAL APPROACH IS ENTIRELY SPONTANEOUS AND UTTERLY UNCOORDINATED."

From the corner, Mo Chrá made a sound that, in a lesser weapon, might have been called a snicker.

CHAPTER 5 SCENE (EXPANDED): THE GHOUL PROBLEM

Part II: Crowd Control

Cian pulled up a new file — incident reports from the last decade. His own handwriting, digitized. Photos that would give crime scene investigators nightmares.

“Let’s table the regeneration problem for now. I’ve got a more immediate concern.”

“PROCEED.”

“Ghouls. Fast ones. The new breed coming out of Eastern Europe — they’re not like the shambling corpses we dealt with in the fourteenth century. These things sprint. They climb walls like insects. And they’re running in packs of thirty, forty at a time.”
He pulled up a surveillance photo — grainy, taken from a rooftop in Bucharest. The image showed a blur of motion scaling the side of a five-story building, limbs bent at angles that suggested either broken bones or joints that no longer worked according to human anatomy.

“Last month, a hunter team in Moldova — six experienced operatives — got swarmed. They had silver rounds, blessed blades, the works. Killed maybe eight before they were overwhelmed. The ghouls didn’t stop to feed. They just… dismantled them. Like they were removing obstacles.”

“THOU DOST SUSPECT COORDINATION.”

“I know there’s coordination. These aren’t mindless corpses anymore, Liaigh. Someone’s breeding them. Training them. Deploying them tactically.” He pulled up another image — claw marks on concrete, arranged in patterns. “See this? They’re marking territory. Communicating. This isn’t instinct. This is military doctrine.”

“THE VAMPIRE.”

“Lord Vârcolac.” Cian’s voice went flat. “Seven centuries he’s been building his army. The thermite rounds are for him — when I finally corner the bastard. But his ghouls are the immediate problem. I can’t fight forty wall-crawlers in close quarters. Not alone. Not even with Mo Chrá.”
From the corner, the sword’s presence shifted — not disagreement, exactly, but something close to reluctant acknowledgment.

“I need area denial. I need crowd control. I need something that kills fast, spreads faster, and doesn’t require me to aim at every single target individually.”

“THOU DOST SEEK THE IMPLEMENTS OF WAR RATHER THAN THE TOOLS OF THE DUEL.”

“Exactly.”

Liaigh was silent for a moment. When he spoke again, his voice had shifted — still empyreal, but with the particular gravity he reserved for matters of tactical significance.

“THEN ATTEND, CHILD OF MORNA, FOR THIS DISCOURSE SHALL REQUIRE PRECISION. GHOULS ARE NOT NEPHILIM. THEY POSSESS NO SPIRITUAL ANCHOR, NO REGENERATIVE MATRIX DERIVED FROM WATCHER ESSENCE. THEY ARE ANIMATED FLESH — CORPSES GIVEN MOTION THROUGH NECROMANTIC BINDING RATHER THAN DIVINE CORRUPTION.”

“So what holds them together?”

“THE BINDING IS THREEFOLD: THE NECROMANTIC SIGIL INSCRIBED UPON THEIR REMAINS, THE VAMPIRIC ESSENCE THAT ANIMATES THEIR MOTOR FUNCTIONS, AND THE PRESERVATION OF SUFFICIENT TISSUE INTEGRITY TO PERMIT LOCOMOTION. DISRUPT ANY ONE OF THESE ELEMENTS, AND THE ANIMATION CEASETH.”

Cian’s mind was already racing. “The sigil’s probably somewhere protected — skull, maybe, or ribcage. Takes too long to target individually in a swarm. Vampiric essence disperses when the body’s destroyed — that’s why fire works. And tissue integrity…”

“CAN BE COMPROMISED THROUGH SUFFICIENT TRAUMA. MECHANICAL, THERMAL, OR CHEMICAL.”

“Mechanical’s too slow against that many targets. I can’t shoot forty ghouls before they close distance. That leaves thermal and chemical.” He turned back to his workbench, pulling up compound databases. “Fire’s traditional, but these new breeds have been observed running through flames to reach targets. They don’t feel pain. They don’t have survival instincts. They’ll burn and keep coming until structural failure drops them.”

“THEN THOU REQUIREST NOT MERELY COMBUSTION BUT ACCELERATED STRUCTURAL FAILURE.”

“Exactly. I need something that doesn’t just burn — I need something that destroys.” He scrolled through chemical formulas. “Give me your assessment. Chlorine trifluoride.”

“AH.” There was something in Liaigh’s voice — not quite approval, but perhaps respect for the audacity of the suggestion. “CLFT — THE OXIDIZER THAT BURNETH SUBSTANCES WHICH OUGHT NOT TO BE COMBUSTIBLE. GLASS. SAND. ASBESTOS. MATERIALS THAT HAVE ALREADY BEEN CONSUMED BY FIRE.”

“It’s hypergolic with almost everything organic. Contact ignition, no external spark required. The Nazis developed it for flamethrowers but found it too dangerous to deploy.”

”‘TOO DANGEROUS FOR THE NAZIS’ IS NOT A RECOMMENDATION, CHILD OF MORNA.”

“It’s a challenge.”

“THY CAPACITY FOR SELF-HARM CONTINUETH TO EXCEED MINE EXPECTATIONS, AND MINE EXPECTATIONS WERE ALREADY CALIBRATED FOR CONSIDERABLE RECKLESSNESS.”

“Can it work?”

Liaigh paused — the kind of pause that meant he was actually considering it rather than dismissing it outright.

“CHLORINE TRIFLUORIDE POSSESSETH THEORETICAL EFFICACY AGAINST UNDEAD TISSUE. UPON CONTACT, IT WOULD INITIATE IMMEDIATE OXIDATION OF ORGANIC MATTER — NOT MERELY COMBUSTION BUT MOLECULAR DISSOLUTION. THE REACTION PRODUCETH HYDROGEN FLUORIDE AS A BYPRODUCT, WHICH WOULD FURTHER COMPROMISE TISSUE INTEGRITY EVEN IN AREAS NOT DIRECTLY CONTACTED.”

“So it spreads.”

“IT DEVASTATETH. HOWEVER—” The word carried the weight of inevitable objection. “THE SAME PROPERTIES THAT RENDER IT EFFECTIVE AGAINST UNDEAD TISSUE RENDER IT CATASTROPHICALLY DANGEROUS TO ITS WIELDER. CLFT CANNOT BE STORED IN MOST CONTAINERS. IT ATTACKETH METAL, GLASS, AND MOST PLASTICS WITH EQUAL ENTHUSIASM. THE SUBSTANCE HATH BEEN DOCUMENTED TO IGNITE CONCRETE AND GRAVEL. ITS REACTION WITH WATER IS EXPLOSIVELY EXOTHERMIC.”

“What about passivated containers? Nickel-lined steel, pre-treated with fluorine gas to form a protective fluoride layer?”

“SUCH CONTAINERS CAN MAINTAIN INTEGRITY FOR LIMITED DURATIONS. BUT THE MOMENT OF DEPLOYMENT REMAINETH PROBLEMATIC. HOW DOST THOU PROPOSE TO DELIVER THE COMPOUND TO THY TARGETS WITHOUT DELIVERING IT SIMULTANEOUSLY TO THYSELF?”

Cian drummed his fingers on the desk. “Pressurized dispersal. Short-range, but I’d only need it for area denial. A perimeter system — motion-triggered canisters that spray a fine mist when ghouls cross a threshold.”

“THOU DOST PROPOSE TO CREATE A MINEFIELD OF SUBSTANCES THAT THE REICH ITSELF DEEMED UNCONTROLLABLE.”

“I propose to create a ghoul-proof perimeter. There’s a difference.”

“THE DIFFERENCE EXISTETH PRIMARILY IN THY OPTIMISM REGARDING OUTCOME.”

From the corner, Mo Chrá made a sound. Not quite a hum — more like a snort.

Cian glanced at the sword. “If you have a better suggestion, I’m listening.”

The sword's presence shifted, and Liaigh translated:

“THE BLADE OBSERVETH THAT THOU DOST OVERCOMPLICATE THE THERMAL APPROACH. IT SUGGESTETH THAT RATHER THAN PURSUING EXOTIC OXIDIZERS OF CATASTROPHIC VOLATILITY, THOU MIGHTEST CONSIDER WEAPONIZING COMPOUNDS THAT ACHIEVE SIMILAR EFFECTS THROUGH LESS… SUICIDAL… MECHANISMS.”

“Such as?”

“DIMETHYL SULFOXIDE COMBINED WITH CAPSAICIN EXTRACT.”

Cian blinked. “DMSO-capsaicin? That’s a pain compliance compound. Ghouls don’t feel pain.”

"THE BLADE IS NOT FINISHED." Liaigh's tone carried the particular patience of someone relaying a message from an entity that was enjoying itself.

“DIMETHYL SULFOXIDE SERVETH AS A CARRIER AGENT — IT PENETRATETH TISSUE BARRIERS WITH REMARKABLE EFFICIENCY. THE PAYLOAD NEED NOT BE CAPSAICIN. 
CONSIDER INSTEAD: DMSO AS DELIVERY MECHANISM FOR SODIUM AZIDE.”

Cian went still.

“Sodium azide. NaN₃. The compound used in automotive airbags.”

“PRECISELY. UPON CONTACT WITH ORGANIC TISSUE, SODIUM AZIDE CONVERTETH TO HYDRAZOIC ACID — A SUBSTANCE THAT DISRUPTETH CELLULAR RESPIRATION AT THE MITOCHONDRIAL LEVEL. FOR LIVING TARGETS, THIS WOULD BE LETHAL BUT SLOW. FOR UNDEAD TISSUE ANIMATED BY VAMPIRIC ESSENCE…”

“The essence requires metabolic processes to maintain animation. Disrupt the metabolism, disrupt the animation.”

“THE BLADE CONCURRETH WITH THY REASONING.”

Cian pulled up a new file and started calculating. “DMSO penetrates tissue at approximately 0.001 centimeters per second. A spray application would achieve full dermal penetration within minutes. If I combine that with—” He stopped, looking at a new formula. “Wait. What about a binary system? Two compounds that are stable separately but react catastrophically when mixed inside the target?”

“PROCEED.”

“Compound A: DMSO carrying sodium hypochlorite. Compound B: DMSO carrying hydrochloric acid. Hit the same target with both, and inside the tissue you get…”

“CHLORINE GAS GENERATION WITHIN THE TARGET’S STRUCTURAL MATRIX.” Liaigh’s voice carried something that might have been grudging approval. “THE REACTION WOULD CAUSE RAPID TISSUE DESTRUCTION FROM THE INTERIOR, COMPROMISING STRUCTURAL INTEGRITY FAR MORE QUICKLY THAN EXTERNAL APPLICATION OF THE SAME COMPOUNDS.”

“And chlorine gas disrupts the necromantic binding?”

“CHLORINE IS A HALOGEN. HALOGENS POSSESS DOCUMENTED EFFICACY AGAINST SUPERNATURAL BONDS — THIS IS WHY SALT CIRCLES FUNCTION AS BARRIERS. CHLORINE GAS, BEING A HALOGEN IN REACTIVE GASEOUS FORM, WOULD ATTACK THE BINDING SIGILS EVEN AS IT DESTROYED THE TISSUE HOUSING THEM.”
Cian was writing furiously now. “So a dual-reservoir spray system. Compound A in one tank, Compound B in the other. The target gets hit with both streams simultaneously. Inside the ghoul, they mix, react, and—”

“THE GHOUL CEASETH TO BE A GHOUL AND BECOMETH A COLLAPSING MASS OF CHEMICALLY COMPROMISED TISSUE RELEASING TOXIC FUMES THAT FURTHER DEGRADE ITS FELLOWS.”

“A chain reaction. One ghoul goes down releasing chlorine, the chlorine damages the ghouls around it, they release more chlorine…”

“THOU HAST CONCEPTUALIZED A CASCADE FAILURE SYSTEM.”

“I’ve conceptualized a ghoul bomb.”

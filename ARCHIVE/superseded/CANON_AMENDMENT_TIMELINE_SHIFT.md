# PART II: RATIONALE

## 1. Real-World Technology Alignment

| Technology | 2022 Reality | 2026 Projection |
|------------|--------------|-----------------|
| **Starship** | Still in testing; no orbital success | Fully mature; orbital refueling proven |
| **Mars Missions** | Perseverance active; private sector nascent | Private sector Mars missions plausible |
| **AI Autonomy** | Advanced but limited | Rover-level AI autonomy credible |

The 2026 launch date is **more believable** for a $1.2B private Mars mission using Starship.

## 2. Mars Window Alignment

Real Mars opposition windows:
- 2022: Oct-Dec (original timeline)
- **2026: Oct-Dec** (new timeline) ← Optimal for story
- 2028: Nov-Jan

The **October 15, 2026** launch date falls within the real 2026 Mars window. Seven-month Hohmann transfer puts arrival at **May 2027**.

## 3. Internal Consistency Resolution

**The Problem (Old Timeline):**
- Chapter 3 set in "2023"
- Titan-1 launched Nov 2022, arrived July 2023
- Chapter 3 takes place ~6 months after arrival
- But Brennan presents data as if mission is well-established

**The Solution (New Timeline):**
- Chapter 3 set in **late 2027**
- Titan-1 arrived May 2027
- Chapter 3 takes place ~6 months after arrival
- Brennan has had time to analyze anomalies, reroute rover, collect data

## 4. Narrative Urgency

Setting the story in **2024-2028** rather than 2020-2024:
- Feels more immediate to readers
- Apocalyptic elements feel more imminent
- Technology assumptions are less speculative

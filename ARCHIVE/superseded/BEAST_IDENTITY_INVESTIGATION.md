## RESOLUTION SUMMARY

The Apollo-Apollyon linguistic connection is the key:
- Ἀπόλλων (Apollo) — Greek god of prophecy, plague, destruction
- Ἀπολλύων (Apollyon) — "The Destroyer" (Revelation 9:11)
- ἀπόλλυμι (apollumi) — root verb meaning "to destroy utterly"

**OHYA** (Shemyaza's firstborn son, documented in the Book of Giants) IS the entity the Greeks remembered as Apollo, and IS the Apollyon of Revelation 9:11.

### Why OHYA, Not Shemyaza:

| Factor | Shemyaza | Ohya |
|--------|----------|------|
| Showed remorse | YES — petitioned for mercy | NO record |
| Expressed shame | YES — couldn't lift eyes to heaven | NO record |
| Nature | Watcher (angel) | Nephilim (hybrid) |
| "Destroyer" title | Not directly applied | Fits — "killed many" |
| Apollo connection | Weak | STRONG — prophecy, plague, beauty |
| Post-death status | Bound angel | Disembodied Nephilim spirit = demon KING |

### The OHYA-Nimrod Possession Cycle:

1. Pre-Flood: Ohya exists in Nephilim body
2. Flood/Nephilim Wars: Ohya's body destroyed; spirit becomes demon
3. Noah's Petition: 90% of Nephilim spirits bound, including Ohya
4. Post-Flood: Ohya's spirit possesses NIMROD via Naamah/Semiramis channel
5. Nimrod's death: Ohya returned to Abyss
6. History: Ohya awaits; Naamah seeks hosts through millennia
7. Revelation 9: Abyss opens; Ohya emerges with locust army
8. End Times: Ohya possesses prepared vessel (soulless transhuman created by Azazel)

## PROBLEMS WITH SHEMYAZA AS APOLLYON

### Problem 1: The "Angel" Designation

**Revelation 9:11:**
> "And they had a king over them, which is the **angel** of the bottomless pit, whose name in the Hebrew tongue is Abaddon."

Shemyaza IS an angel, so this seems to fit. However...

### Problem 2: Shemyaza's Recorded Repentance/Anguish

**1 Enoch 13:3-6:**
> "They were all afraid, and fear and trembling seized them... they besought me to draw up a petition for them that they might find forgiveness... they could not speak nor lift up their eyes to heaven **for shame of their sins**."

**1 Enoch 14:5-7 (God's response):**
> "Ye shall have no peace nor forgiveness of sin... mercy and peace shall ye not attain."

**Book of Giants (4Q530):**
Shemyaza's "heart was troubled" when his sons Ohya and Hahya reported prophetic nightmares of coming judgment.

**The Question:** Does someone who petitioned for mercy (even if denied) fit the role of ultimate villain?

### Problem 3: Terror vs. Defiance

The Watchers are portrayed as:
- Afraid
- Ashamed  
- Unable to face heaven
- Begging for intercession

This is a far cry from the Beast who:
- Makes war with the saints
- Speaks blasphemies against God
- Demands worship
- Shows no remorse

### Problem 4: Narrative Complexity

Making Shemyaza the Beast flattens him into "pure evil." But the textual evidence suggests a more tragic figure — a commander who fell through obsession (Naamah), led others into sin, and later experienced genuine anguish when judgment came.

## ALTERNATIVE CANDIDATE: NIMROD

### Biblical Foundation

**Genesis 10:8-9:**
> "And Cush begat **Nimrod**: he began to be a mighty one (גִּבּוֹר / gibbor) in the earth. He was a mighty hunter **before** the LORD."

**Critical Hebrew Analysis:**

The phrase "began to be" uses **חָלַל** (chalal) — which can mean:
- To begin
- To profane
- To defile
- To pollute

Alternative translation: "Nimrod **profaned himself into becoming** a gibbor."

This suggests a TRANSFORMATION — possibly demonic possession or genetic corruption.

### Nimrod's Resume

| Attribute | Evidence |
|-----------|----------|
| **Founder of Babel** | Genesis 10:10 — "the beginning of his kingdom was Babel" |
| **Builder of Babylon** | The anti-God system from Genesis to Revelation |
| **Post-Flood Rebellion** | Chose defiance WITH FULL KNOWLEDGE of the Flood judgment |
| **Tower of Babel** | Jewish tradition credits Nimrod as the organizer |
| **"Against the LORD"** | Hebrew "before" (לִפְנֵי) can mean "in opposition to" |
| **Gibbor Status** | Same word used for Nephilim — suggests hybrid nature |
| **No Repentance** | Unlike Shemyaza, no record of petition, shame, or anguish |

### The Babylon Connection

**Revelation 17:5:**
> "MYSTERY, BABYLON THE GREAT, THE MOTHER OF HARLOTS"

**Revelation 17:8:**
> "The beast that thou sawest **was, and is not**; and shall ascend out of the bottomless pit"

If Nimrod FOUNDED Babylon, and "Mystery Babylon" is the end-times enemy system, then:
- Nimrod's spirit = the animating force of Babylon through history
- His spirit bound in the Abyss = "is not"
- His release = "shall ascend"
- The Beast IS the Babylon system personified

### Nimrod as Apollyon

| Factor | Assessment |
|--------|------------|
| "Angel of the Abyss" | Greek ἄγγελος = "messenger" — not exclusively celestial. A Nephilim king could be the Abyss's agent |
| "Abaddon/Apollyon" = Destroyer | Nimrod the hunter, the conqueror, the rebel fits "Destroyer" |
| Commands locust army | As king of Nephilim spirits (now demons), he commands the Abyss horde |
| Post-Flood defiance | Unlike Watchers who showed fear, Nimrod rebelled with eyes open |
| Babylon founder | Direct connection to Revelation's "Mystery Babylon" |

## REVISED FRAMEWORK PROPOSAL

### Option A: Nimrod as Apollyon/Beast (Recommended)

| Entity | Identity | Role |
|--------|----------|------|
| **The Dragon** | Satan | Empowers all below |
| **Apollyon/The Beast** | NIMROD | King of Nephilim demons; Babylon personified |
| **Shemyaza** | Watcher General | Released at Rev 9, commands Watcher forces |
| **Azazel** | False Prophet | Deceptive teachings, technology, false signs |
| **Naamah** | Whore of Babylon | Human face of the system; goddess cult network |

**Advantages:**
- Shemyaza retains complexity (tragic fallen commander)
- Nimrod's unrepentant rebellion fits "Beast" better
- Babylon connection becomes literal, not symbolic
- Two command structures: Watchers (Shemyaza) + Nephilim spirits (Nimrod)
- Revelation 9 releases BOTH factions

### Option B: Keep Shemyaza as Beast

Retain original framework, accepting that:
- His petition was denied = no redemption
- His anguish was self-pity, not true repentance
- He remains the supreme enemy

**Disadvantages:**
- Flattens a potentially complex character
- Ignores textual evidence of his shame/fear
- Misses the Nimrod-Babylon connection

### Option C: Dual Authority Structure

Both Shemyaza AND Nimrod emerge as co-rulers:
- Shemyaza: Commander of Watcher forces (angels)
- Nimrod/Apollyon: Commander of Nephilim forces (demons)
- They cooperate but represent different factions

**The "Beast" as seen by humans could be:**
- Nimrod's spirit possessing a human vessel (the visible antichrist)
- Empowered by BOTH Shemyaza's Watchers AND Nimrod's demon army

## THE SHEMYAZA QUESTION: TRAGIC OR EVIL?

### Evidence for Tragedy:
- Fell through love/obsession (Naamah), not pure malice
- Petitioned for forgiveness
- Showed shame and fear
- Heart "troubled" over his sons' fate
- Led others into sin but didn't act alone

### Evidence Against Redemption:
- Petition denied by God — implying insufficient repentance
- "Delighted in their children" — continued in sin
- Bound for judgment in fire (1 Enoch 10:13)
- No indication of actual turning from evil

### Narrative Possibility:

Shemyaza could be portrayed as:
- A tragic commander who made a catastrophic choice
- Genuinely regretful but past the point of redemption
- Still dangerous (will fight when released) but not cartoonishly evil
- Contrasted with Nimrod's defiant, unrepentant rebellion

This makes for richer storytelling than pure villain status.

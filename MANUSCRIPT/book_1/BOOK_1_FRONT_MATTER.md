## PAGE 9: AUTHOR'S PREFACE

*[Optional — recommended for this series given the theological material. Short: one page maximum. Recto.]*

&nbsp;

**A Word Before the Story**

&nbsp;

This is a work of fiction. It is also, in the deepest sense I know how to mean it, a work of conviction.

The events described in the pages that follow did not happen. The characters — with certain historical and scriptural exceptions — are invented. But the framework beneath the fiction is not invented. The Book of Enoch, the Book of Jubilees, the Book of Giants recovered from the caves at Qumran — these documents exist. The twenty Watcher chiefs are named in them. Their crimes are catalogued. Their punishments are recorded. The epistle of Jude quotes 1 Enoch directly, which means that what you are about to read draws on material the writers of the New Testament considered worth referencing.

I have taken that material seriously. Where the texts are clear, the novel follows them. Where they are ambiguous, I have made a choice and held to it. Where I have invented — the technology, the sword, the acoustic paradigm, the specific shape of the modern conspiracy — I have tried to invent in a way that is consistent with the logic of what the texts actually say.

This is Christian apocalyptic fiction. It operates from a conviction that the resurrection of Jesus Christ was a real event in history, that angelic and demonic beings are real, and that the war described in scripture is ongoing. Readers who do not share those convictions are still welcome here. The story works as story. But readers who do share them may find it works as something more.

The outcome of the war was decided at Calvary. Everything that follows is the final chapters.

&nbsp;

*— Kerman Gild*

# TYPESETTING NOTES

*[Remove this section before sending to typesetter — internal guidance only]*

**Page numbering:**
- Front matter: Roman numerals (i, ii, iii...) — standard convention
- Half-title and copyright page carry no displayed page number
- Body text begins at Arabic numeral 1 with the Prologue

**Running headers (suggested):**
- Verso (left page): THE NEPHILIM CHRONICLES
- Recto (right page): THE CYDONIAN OATHS
- No running header on chapter opening pages

**Fonts to discuss with typesetter:**
- Display (title, chapter headers): Something with weight — Trajan Pro, Cinzel, or a custom serif with the right antiquity feel
- Body text: 11–11.5pt Garamond, Caslon, or Palatino with 14–15pt leading — gives the text room to breathe at a comfortable reading size
- Minimum body text size: 10.5pt — do not go below this for paperback
- Section breaks within chapters: centred triple asterisk (⁂) or the caduceus-variant symbol from the cover

**Trim size:**
- Recommend 6" × 9" for a book of this length — gives 11pt body text at comfortable line width without feeling cramped
- 5.5" × 8.5" is acceptable but will push smaller text for word count

**Bleed and margins:**
- Inside margin (gutter): minimum 0.75" for a paperback of this page count — spine flex requires it
- Outside margin: 0.5"–0.625"
- Top and bottom: 0.75"

**Cover back blurb (from design review):**
- Minimum 10pt, recommend 11pt Montserrat or Raleway (sans-serif holds better at small sizes in print)
- Left-align body paragraph; keep headline centred
- Lighten the teal box background or replace with semi-transparent dark gradient
- Current blurb is approximately 85 words — recommend expanding to 150–180 words for full back cover presence

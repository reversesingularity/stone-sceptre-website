## PURPOSE

This document maps all twenty Watcher chiefs to their antediluvian
domains, modern geographic positions, House organisations, and
accessibility tiers. It consolidates every reference from the Book 1
published manuscript, Book 2 chapters 1–4, and the Prologues into a
single registry to prevent geographic drift before the Antarctic
chapters begin.

---

## CORE GEOGRAPHY RULE

Before the Flood, the earth was a single landmass (Book 1, Naamah's
study: "what modern geologists dimly intuited when they theorised
about Pangaea, though they placed it hundreds of millions of years too
early"). The twenty chiefs divided this unified territory among
themselves. The Flood fractured the landmass; continental plates
accelerated catastrophically. The twenty steles scattered with the
continents. Modern positions reflect post-cataclysmic drift, not
original proximity.

**Implication:** Two chiefs whose antediluvian domains were adjacent
may now be on opposite sides of the planet.

## CORE PRINCIPLE — THE COGNITIVE FOSSIL

Cian's Iron Age baseline is never erased. It is buried beneath 2,600 years of accumulated doctrine, but it surfaces under stress, in unguarded internal monologue, and in his instinctive pre-combat behaviours.

**Always present in his POV:**
- Thinks in lines, formation, and ground (Iron Age baseline)
- Checks flanks before seeking cover — pre-gunpowder instinct
- Notes exit routes before entry points (millennium-level SERE)
- "Shot" surfaces in internal monologue for ammunition
- Weapon selection is doctrinal, never emotional: minimum effective system for the threat profile

**Voice in combat prose:**
> *"Should read like a Roman general who learned CQB in Fallujah. The ancient professional overlay is always present beneath the modern doctrine. Never write him as a pure modern operator — the Iron Age baseline must be felt."*

## ERA-SPECIFIC INSTINCT OVERLAPS

*[Agent 8 to populate with specific examples drawn from chapter combat sequences]*

### Iron Age → Modern Overlay
- **Flank check before cover:** Pre-gunpowder. Flanking with spears was lethal faster than frontal assault. The instinct is 2,600 years old and entirely automatic.
- **Exit before entry:** A warrior who enters a space without knowing the exits is already dead — applicable from a roundhouse to a modern building.
- **Ground awareness:** Thinking in terrain control, not real estate. Which ground is defensible? Which is a kill zone? This is formation doctrine, not CQB.

### Roman → Modern Overlay
- **Discipline of the line:** Cian doesn't spray. He fires with Roman volley precision — measured, collective, devastating. One shot creates a gap; the gap is exploited.
- **Siege patience:** Prepared to wait. Prepared to absorb a long engagement. Modern operators train for this; Cian *is* this.

### Medieval → Modern Overlay
- **Multi-system fluency:** By 1400 CE, Cian had internalised 12+ distinct weapons systems from 8+ different cultures. Modern weapons are just additional tools in an already vast catalogue.
- **Mounted doctrine:** Shadow Hound combat is cavalry — his instincts for speed, flanking, and disengagement from mounted position are 1,000 years old.

### Cold War → Modern Overlay
- **Tradecraft:** Cian is naturally surveillance-aware. He does not enter a space without a counter-surveillance scan. This pre-dates Cold War doctrine by 2,500 years but Cold War formalised the language.

# Chapter 4 — The Same Word

## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Chapter Highlight:** Cian's Hebrew word study briefing — standing before the team in Austin, displaying three Hebrew words on a screen, revealing that Genesis 10:8 does not say Nimrod "began" to be mighty but was "profaned into" becoming a gibbor. The Nephilim persistence is linguistic.

---

### Prompt

A cinematic interior of a military-style briefing room with scarred oak table and uncomfortable chairs. A tall, weathered man with ancient, knowing eyes stands at a display wall, his hand raised toward a large projected screen showing three Hebrew words in bold white calligraphy against a dark background — angular, ancient script glowing with scholarly authority. Behind the Hebrew text, a faint world map shows twenty golden points of light. The team is seated around the table — a young man with glasses and a laptop, an engineer with a legal pad, a woman at a terminal with two screens showing encrypted data, a soldier leaning against the back wall watching faces not data. The room is austere, underground, lit by the cool blue-white of the projection and overhead fluorescent strips. The atmosphere is a graduate seminar held in a bunker — intellectual revelation delivered with combat urgency. The man at the wall speaks the Hebrew from memory with the authority of someone who learned it when the ink was new. Photorealistic. Tom Clancy meets Dead Sea Scrolls. No text or lettering.

---

### Style Notes

- **Mood:** Theological revelation as tactical intelligence, ancient text as weapon
- **Palette:** Cool projection blue-white, warm amber overhead, golden map points
- **Composition:** Cian at display wall right, team seated left, Hebrew text centre
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

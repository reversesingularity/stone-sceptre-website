# Map 6 — The Operational Arc of Book Two
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Map Description:** A global map showing the full sequence of operations across Book 2 — Austin to Vatican to Galway to Carpathians to Greenland to Mars to Antarctica to Stewart Island, NZ. Naamah's parallel trajectory overlaid.

---

### Prompt

A stylised global operations map on a dark background, rendered as a modern military command-display aesthetic with clean vector lines on a deep navy-black surface. The globe is shown in an oblique projection that reveals both hemispheres. A golden route line traces the protagonist team's operational arc across the surface: Austin, Texas (starting node, golden circle) → Vatican City → Galway, Ireland (major hub, larger circle) → Carpathian Mountains → return to Galway → Greenland (gate node, with a line extending OFF the globe toward a small inset of Mars in the upper corner) → return to Earth → Antarctica → Stewart Island, New Zealand (final node). Each location is marked with a tactical waypoint symbol and a chapter number. In contrasting blood-red, Naamah's parallel trajectory is overlaid: the Ashtoreth's position in the Ross Sea, the drilling operation, the breach, the departure south. The red and gold lines converge at Antarctica — the point where both arcs meet. The Josephite deployment from Christchurch is shown as a separate green line converging on the same point. Mars is shown as an inset globe in the upper right corner with the Noctis Labyrinthus location marked. The aesthetic is military command centre — clean, precise, the kind of display that Chris Guilder would build. Dark background, luminous route lines. No real-world modern labels beyond location markers.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** Global scope of the war, two converging arcs, command-centre precision
- **Palette:** Deep navy-black background, golden protagonist route, blood-red antagonist route, green Josephite line
- **Composition:** Globe centre, golden arc sweeping across, red arc in Antarctic, Mars inset upper right
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

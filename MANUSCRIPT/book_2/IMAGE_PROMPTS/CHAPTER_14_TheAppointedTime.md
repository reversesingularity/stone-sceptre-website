# Chapter 14 — The Appointed Time
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Chapter Highlight:** Azazel walks free. Raphael's seal breaks after five thousand years. The False Prophet climbs the drill shaft to the surface without equipment, without haste. Naamah's vessel waits. He boards. They sail south into the storm. The patience of five millennia considers another ten minutes acceptable.

---

### Prompt

A monumental, terrifying painting of a figure emerging from a massive industrial drill shaft onto the Antarctic ice shelf surface. The figure is tall, inhumanly beautiful, radiating an aura of ancient, patient intelligence — not monstrous but *wrong* in the way perfection can be wrong, the beauty of something that taught humanity how to destroy itself. He wears nothing appropriate for the Antarctic cold and is unaffected by it. His skin is pale, his features sharp and symmetrical, his eyes carrying the weight of five thousand years of confinement and the absolute absence of hurry. Behind him, the drill shaft descends into darkness, its steel casing rimed with ice, industrial equipment abandoned around its perimeter. Before him, across a stretch of wind-scoured ice shelf, a dark research vessel sits in the pack ice — the Ashtoreth — its lights burning, a figure visible on deck watching his approach. The Antarctic sky is a churning wall of storm — dark clouds spiralling, wind whipping ice crystals horizontally. The False Prophet walks toward the ship with the measured stride of a being for whom five thousand years was merely preparation. The scale is vast — tiny figure against infinite ice and sky. Sublime horror. Caspar David Friedrich meets Revelation. No text or lettering.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** The patience of evil, sublime Antarctic scale, the point of no return
- **Palette:** Antarctic whites and greys, dark storm sky, warm vessel lights, pale inhuman figure
- **Composition:** Azazel walking centre, drill shaft behind, vessel ahead, storm above, vast ice plain
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

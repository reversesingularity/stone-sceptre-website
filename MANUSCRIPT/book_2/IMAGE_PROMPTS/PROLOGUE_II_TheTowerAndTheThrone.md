# Prologue II — The Tower and the Throne

## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Chapter Highlight:** Naamah's extraction experiment at Babel — Ohya's disembodied spirit speaking through Nimrod's body on a cedar chair in a secret chamber beneath the throne room, while the Tower of Babel rises in the background.

---

### Prompt

A dark, atmospheric painting of a secret underground chamber lit by oil lamps and faint violet-grey phosphorescent inscriptions on the walls. A massive, 9 ft muscular dark-skinned man sits rigidly on a plain cedar chair in the centre of the chamber, his eyes glowing with an ancient, intelligence that does not match his young powerful body — he is Nimrod. Standing at the edge of the chamber, partially in shadow, a breathtakingly beautiful woman, long blonde hair, in dark robes watches him with clinical detachment, her posture erect, arms folded, the posture of a surgeon observing a procedure. Her face is illuminated by the phosphorescent glow — ageless, sharp, emotionless. Above them, visible through a shaft or architectural opening, the half-built Tower of Babel rises against a Mesopotamian night sky — zigzag tiers of baked brick reaching impossibly upward, construction fires burning on the upper platforms. The mood is intimate, conspiratorial, dangerous. Oil paint texture. Rembrandt lighting. No text or lettering.

---

### Style Notes

- **Mood:** Occult intimacy, possession, maternal calculation
- **Palette:** Deep amber oil-lamp warmth, grey-violet phosphorescence, night sky above
- **Composition:** Nimrod/Ohya centre on chair, Naamah standing at left edge in shadow, tower visible above
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

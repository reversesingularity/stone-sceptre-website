# Map 5 — Dudael: The Prison Beneath the Ice
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Map Description:** Cross-section of the Dudael prison complex beneath the Ross Ice Shelf — Naamah's drill shaft from surface through ice and rock, the repurposed Gadreel fortress, Raphael's inverted ward architecture, the corridors and kill-boxes, and the central seal chamber where Azazel was contained.

---

### Prompt

A dramatic vertical cross-section illustration showing the full depth from the Antarctic surface to the prison below. At the top: the Ross Ice Shelf surface, flat and white, with the research vessel Ashtoreth visible as a small dark silhouette on the ice. A drill shaft descends vertically through layers — first through white-blue Antarctic ice (labelled depths), then through dark sub-glacial basalt rock, then into Cydonian metamaterial — the ancient dark stone of Gadreel's fortress repurposed by Raphael as a prison. The fortress architecture is rendered in cutaway: outer corridors showing Raphael's inverted ward inscriptions (wards that face inward, containing rather than protecting), narrowing kill-box corridors marked with violet ward-emitter positions, defensive chokepoints, and at the very bottom — the central seal chamber. The seal chamber is a perfect geometric void, circular, the walls covered in dense golden acoustic inscriptions — Raphael's sustained note made visible as carved frequency patterns. At the chamber's centre, an empty space where the prisoner was held — now vacant, the inscriptions faded, the seal broken. Cracks radiate outward from the empty centre. The overall impression is of depth — 2.7 miles of vertical descent from the mundane surface to the supernatural prison. Geological illustration meets sacred architecture. No real-world modern labels.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** Abyssal depth, divine engineering repurposed as prison, the weight of what was contained here
- **Palette:** White ice top, dark basalt middle, Cydonian black-violet bottom, golden seal inscriptions, faded/cracked centre
- **Composition:** Vertical cross-section, surface at top, prison at bottom, drill shaft connecting
- **Aspect Ratio:** 9:16 portrait (vertical depth emphasis)

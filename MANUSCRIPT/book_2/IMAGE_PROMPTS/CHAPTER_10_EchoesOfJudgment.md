# Chapter 10 — Echoes of Judgment
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Chapter Highlight:** The annihilation playback in the Araqiel bunker's inner chambers — the team discovers what Michael's destroying angels did to the Nephilim families who hid on Mars. Every ward has been surgically shattered. No remains. The wards were not overwhelmed — they were *read* and unmade. This was not a siege. It was an audit.

---

### Prompt

A haunting, solemn painting of a vast underground chamber carved from dark alien stone, every surface covered in geometric ward inscriptions that have been surgically broken — clean, precise interruptions in the glowing lines where each frequency pattern simply stops, as though erased by something that understood each ward perfectly. The chamber was once a nursery and family quarters — empty cribs carved from stone, overturned furniture, abandoned possessions scattered across the floor, but no bodies, no remains, no evidence of violence except the systematic dismantling of every protective ward. A warrior enters from the left, holding a faintly glowing teal sword forward like a torch, its light revealing the scope of the destruction. Behind him, a soldier sweeps the space with a rifle, and a woman stands frozen, one hand on her katana, staring at an empty stone crib. The ward-breaks glow with residual white-gold light — the ghost signature of the original creation frequency that unmade them. The mood is not horror but something worse: the silence of a judgment so precise it left nothing to mourn. Cathedral-scale interior. Solemn. Sacred devastation. No text or lettering.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** Sacred devastation, divine judgment as forensic precision, the absence that is worse than carnage
- **Palette:** Dark stone, teal sword-light, white-gold residual ward-breaks, shadow
- **Composition:** Warrior entering from left with sword-light, empty nursery centre, broken wards on all surfaces
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

# Chapter 12 — The Josephite Gambit
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Chapter Highlight:** Chris Guilder alone in the Galway signals lab — three screens saying SIGNAL LOST, the team on Mars unreachable, while the Ross Sea feeds show Naamah has breached the prison. He takes executive command of the entire Guardian Network from a desk. Courage that looks like spreadsheets.

---

### Prompt

A cinematic interior of a signals laboratory at night, lit only by the glow of multiple computer screens. A young man with glasses sits alone at a three-monitor arc, his face illuminated by the screens. The three primary monitors display two stark words in white on black: SIGNAL LOST. But surrounding them, a secondary ring of screens shows a constellation of active intelligence feeds — satellite imagery of an Antarctic vessel, financial transaction networks with highlighted wire transfers, a helicopter transponder blinking on a flight-path overlay toward the Ross Ice Shelf, encrypted communication channels. The young man's posture is not defeated — it is the controlled stillness of someone who has accepted that he cannot reach the people he sent into danger and has redirected every faculty toward the war he CAN fight from this desk. His hands rest on the keyboard, steady. A coffee cup sits untouched. The room is otherwise empty — one chair, one operator, the weight of an entire network's command structure resting on a twenty-nine-year-old who was rejected by Google three times. The fluorescent overhead is off. Only screen-light. The loneliest command post in the world. Photorealistic. No text or lettering.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** Solitary command, quiet courage, the war that looks like data analysis
- **Palette:** Screen-blue and white on the face, surrounding darkness, green/amber secondary feeds
- **Composition:** Chris centre in screen-light, primary monitors above, secondary feeds surrounding, darkness beyond
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

# Map 3 — The Greenland Gate
## DALL-E / Microsoft Designer Image Prompt

**Map Description:** Site map of the Isua Greenstone Belt nunatak in southwest Greenland — showing the Gulfstream landing at Kangerlussuaq, Storm Herald helicopter transit, the gate structure in ancient Archean gneiss, the House Satar disaster site, and Michael's territorial interdict radius.

---

### Prompt

A detailed site map of a remote Greenland location rendered in Arctic cartographic style — white and pale blue ice fields, dark exposed rock formations (nunataks), and the deep indigo of fjord water. The map shows a transit route: a small aircraft symbol at an airfield (Kangerlussuaq), connected by a dashed helicopter flight-path line to a nunatak formation in the Isua Greenstone Belt. The nunatak is rendered in geological detail — 3.8-billion-year-old dark Archean gneiss, the oldest exposed rock on Earth. At its centre, a structure is marked with a golden geometric sigil — the planar gate, carved into the ancient stone. Nearby, a red hazard zone marks the House Satar disaster site — destroyed surveillance equipment icons, casualty markers. Surrounding the gate at a defined radius, a shimmering golden circle marks Michael's territorial interdict boundary — the zone within which Watcher-tainted biology cannot survive. The interdict circle radiates faint golden light. Searchlight positions are marked around the gate perimeter. The polar darkness is conveyed through deep blue-black sky tones at the map edges. Arctic expedition cartography meets supernatural operational planning. No real-world modern labels.

---

### Style Notes
- **Mood:** Arctic isolation, ancient geology containing alien architecture, divine jurisdiction marked on a map
- **Palette:** Arctic whites, pale blues, dark gneiss grey-black, golden interdict circle, red hazard zone
- **Composition:** Helicopter route from top-left, nunatak centre, gate and interdict circle at centre, disaster site nearby
- **Aspect Ratio:** 16:9 landscape

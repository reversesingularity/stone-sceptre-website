### BEAT 1: "THE CLAIMING" — The Fianna Misunderstanding

**Setting:** C-130J cargo bay, improvised medical station. Cian regaining consciousness.
**POV:** Cian

**Setup (established at end of Ch6):**
At the Carpathian extraction, Miriam placed Mo Chra across Cian's chest on the medical sled — practical space management. The blade was too long to strap alongside the stretcher, the cargo bay was tight, and Cian's hands were the safest cradle for a weapon that objected to being handled by anyone else.

**The Iron Age Layer:**
In Fianna tradition, a woman placing a warrior's sword across his chest is a formal declaration of guardianship — *bean-choimhdeachta* (woman-guardian). Not a marriage proposal. Something more intimate: a public claim that this warrior's life is under her protection, that she will answer for his honour while he cannot answer for himself. Only a woman of equal standing could make this declaration. The last woman who did this for Cian was Siobhan. In 1170 CE. 855 years ago.

**The Waking — Sequence:**

1. Cian surfaces in layers — first the acoustic environment (C-130 engines, heartbeats), then physical pain (the Chains cost), then the weight of the sword across his sternum
2. **TERROR:** Mo Chra is silent. For the first time in 2,600 years, the sword is not humming. No presence. No frequency. Total acoustic void. The Chains of Abyssal Damnation cost the sword everything
3. His eyes open. He sees the blade placement. His Iron Age substrate — the same cognitive fossil that overrode Cold War doctrine in the hexagonal chamber — short-circuits
4. 850 years of carefully maintained emotional fortifications experience catastrophic structural failure
5. He tries to speak in formal Old Irish — the register of acceptance. He gets approximately four words in before Miriam, checking his pupil dilation with a penlight, says "Hold still, your left pupil is sluggish"
6. She has NO idea what she's done. She is running triage. She is a GCHQ analyst performing field medical assessment. She is NOT making an Iron Age declaration of guardianship
7. **KEY:** Miriam has reverted to "Mac Morna." The Ch6 first-name break was involuntary, under duress, and she has re-erected her GCHQ composure walls. Cian hears "Mac Morna" and processes two things simultaneously: relief (she's professional, it was just medical triage) and devastating disappointment (she's professional, it was just medical triage)

**The Comedy Engine:**

- Cian keeps trying to formally respond to a proposal that hasn't been made
- Every attempt is interrupted by a medical procedure — blood pressure cuff, IV adjustment, pupil check
- His formal Old Irish keeps colliding with modern medical terminology
- He is simultaneously the most dangerous mortal combatant in history and a man whose 2,636 years of experience have provided zero protocol for this exact scenario

**Mo Chra's First Sign of Life:**

- When Miriam tries to move the sword to access Cian's chest for heart rate monitoring, Mo Chra becomes impossibly heavy — a reflex, not conscious matchmaking. The sword's depleted acoustic reserves produced one instinctive response: *do not separate me from my wielder*
- This is the first sign she's alive. Cian's relief is physical — his hand closes around the hilt and he feels the faintest warmth. Not a hum. A heartbeat
- Miriam notices the weight shift. Files it. Says nothing. Adjusts her approach and monitors from the wrist instead

- He procures:

  - Warm food — whatever the McNeeve galley has at this hour
- He brings these to her. He does not sit down. He stands in the corridor, too sore to bend easily but too stubborn to show it, and delivers the most ancient form of male devotion: *I have provided for you. Now rest*

**The Line:**

> "You held the line for me. Now let me hold it for you."

- This is NOT "I love you." Cian mac Morna does not say "I love you" in Book 2. He says it in actions. In Iron Age idiom. In the language of a man who has survived four wives, sworn never to love again, and is watching that oath crumble in the corridor of a medical facility at 3 AM while holding a cup of tea he made for a woman who carries a katana

**Miriam's Response:**

- Her walls crack. Not the catastrophic failure of the battlefield — that was panic, adrenaline, amygdala overriding prefrontal cortex. This is different. This is *exhaustion*
- This is the moment when you have been running for twenty years and someone says *stop running. I'm here*
- She does not cry. Miriam Ashford does not cry. She simply stops fighting the tiredness
- She takes the blanket. She takes the tea. She sits in the chair and her eyes close and she is asleep within minutes
- **She says "Mac Morna" one more time before sleep — a reflex, not a choice. The GCHQ wall holds. For now**

**The Watch:**

- Cian does not sleep. He stands in the corridor and watches the door
- Mo Chra hums a soft, protective lullaby — the same frequency she produced when Siobhan slept, 850 years ago. This is the sword's way of saying: *I remember. I approve. I will keep watch too*
- Mo Chra's lullaby is the first sustained frequency she's produced since the Chains. She spent her first recovered energy not on combat diagnostics, not on ward analysis, but on *this*

**Chapter Close:**

- The medical bay is quiet. James doing weapons maintenance. Brennan running Armaros stele data through acoustic analysis software. Chris Guilder asleep in a chair with laptop still open
- Miriam sleeps. Cian watches the door. Mo Chra hums
- In Cian's mind, Liaigh speaks one final line before withdrawing:

> "WELL DONE."

- Two words. Empyreal Register. No elaboration
- The most dangerous mortal combatant in history has finally done something that the Archangel Raphael considers genuinely brave
- **Chapter ends**

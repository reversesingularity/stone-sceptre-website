### BEAT 2: "THE LABYRINTH" — First Steps on Mars
**Setting:** Noctis Labyrinthus canyon system. Interior of the Araqiel bunker entry corridor.
**POV:** Brennan primary (scientific awe), Cian secondary (tactical sweep)

**The Environment:**
- Noctis Labyrinthus: a vast system of steep-walled valleys at the western terminus of Valles Marineris. Trenches up to 6km deep, carved by tectonic fracturing and subsurface collapse. The most geologically complex terrain on Mars
- The entry point is in a canyon wall, ~3km below the rim. The Titan-3 rover sits outside, its cameras still transmitting (the last footage Brennan's team on Earth will receive before the transit window closes and the gate's acoustic interference drowns the signal)
- Through the gate: a corridor of Cydonian metamaterial. Similar to the Armaros domain descent in Ch6, but fundamentally different in character:
  - Armaros built wards — containment, binding, the craft of a being who understood covenants. His architecture was *designed to keep things out*
  - Araqiel built escape routes — navigation, cartography, the craft of a being who understood terrain. His architecture is *designed to move through quickly*. The corridors are wide. The ceilings are high. The geometry is linear, not spiral. This was built by a being who was mapping exit vectors, not defensive positions
  - The difference tells the story before the team finds any records: this was not a fortress. This was a *refuge*

**Brennan's Scientific Response:**
- Brennan mac Niamh, Mars mission lead, the man who spent years preparing Titan rovers for this world, is now standing on it. Not in an EVA suit. Not through a rover's camera. *Standing on Mars*
- He touches the wall. Cydonian metamaterial — the same material his rovers photographed at Cydonia, the same material Mo Chra is made of, the same material that witnessed the Watchers' oath. But here, on Mars, it is *home*. This is where it was quarried. This is where it was shaped. Every piece of Cydonian ore on Earth — Mo Chra, the twenty steles, the gate — is an exile. This is origin
- His engineering mind is already cataloguing: atmospheric composition (breathable — nitrogen/oxygen mix maintained by Watcher life-support infrastructure, not Martian ambient), pressure (sea-level equivalent), temperature (18°C — comfortable, maintained for 5,000 years by self-sustaining systems), structural integrity (excellent — Cydonian metamaterial does not degrade; it was built to outlast planets)
- His emotional response is harder to catalogue. This is the validation of everything — the Titan programme, the data he nearly died to protect, the coordinates that cost lives. It is all real. It was always real

**The Bunker's Character:**
- As the team moves deeper, the bunker reveals its nature. This is not the disciplined geometry of a military installation. This is the architecture of *panic*:
  - Storage chambers crammed with supplies (desiccated after 5 millennia but identifiable: food stores, water recycling equipment, medical facilities)
  - Living quarters — not barracks, but *family quarters*. The Araqiel clan brought their families. Women. Children. They thought they could wait out the Flood on another planet
  - A nursery. Small sleeping alcoves. Cian sees it and stops walking. He has killed 50,000 supernatural entities. He has cleared passage tombs and bunkers and fortresses. He has never found a nursery in a Watcher installation. These were not just warriors. They were people who had children and were trying to save them
  - Communication equipment — long-range acoustic transmitters pointed at Earth. They were listening. They heard the Flood. They heard the screams. And then they heard nothing
- The acoustic architecture shifts as they go deeper. The outer corridors are navigation-optimised — Araqiel's cartographic genius applied to escape routing. But the inner chambers carry a different signature: *fear*. The Cydonian ore has absorbed the emotional acoustic imprint of beings who knew they were hiding from something that could not be hidden from

**James's Assessment:**
- James Madigan clears each room with the mechanical precision of thirty years of CQB. But his rhythm is off. The nursery did it. James has a daughter. Cian knows this. Neither says anything
- James's tactical assessment, delivered in the flat operational register that means he is managing something he will not discuss: "No hostiles. No remains. No signs of combat in these sections. Whatever happened, it didn't happen here. It happened deeper"

**Miriam's Observation:**
- Miriam is cataloguing. Victoria Ashford's daughter, trained to read money trails like maps, is now reading the material culture of an alien refugee camp. She photographs everything. She cross-references against the Araqiel sigil from the twenty-voice round

- The gate is a 20-minute traverse back through the bunker corridors (which are now acoustically hostile). The gate activation requires Mo Chra's participation — she must produce the transit frequency to lock the new destination (redirect from Earth-origin to Antarctica-destination)
- If Mo Chra cannot function, they cannot transit. If they cannot transit in 90 minutes, the standing wave collapses and they are stranded on Mars
- Brennan delivers this assessment with the calm of an engineer reporting a structural failure: factual, precise, terrified

**Cian's Command Decision:**
- Cian does what he has done for 2,600 years when Mo Chra will not cooperate: he argues with her
- This is not the internal monologue of a man talking to a sword. This is a 2,600-year relationship under extreme stress. He speaks aloud — James, Miriam, and Brennan hear him addressing the blade directly:

> "I know what you heard. I heard it too. But we are not staying here to grieve for a clan that died five thousand years ago. We have a prison to reach and a False Prophet to stop and sixty minutes to do it in. So you will FOCUS, or I will sheathe you and navigate by torchlight, and you know how well that went in Constantinople."

- Mo Chra responds. A pulse. Faint. Grudging. The sword's equivalent of a soldier being pulled back to duty from shock
- The Constantinople reference: a shared memory. A time Cian tried to operate without the sword and nearly died. The sword remembers. The sword does not want a repeat
- Mo Chra's hum returns — weak, fragmented, but functional. She can produce the transit frequency. She can navigate. She is not recovered, but she is *operational*

**The Retreat:**
- The team moves back through the destabilising bunker at double-time. James on point (rifle, not sword — the corridors are wide enough for firearms). Cian with Mo Chra providing acoustic navigation through the ghost frequencies. Brennan counting minutes. Miriam covering the rear with the Muromachi katana drawn — not because threats are expected, but because the disorienting acoustics make her instincts scream *danger* and the blade in her hands is the only thing keeping her moving forward instead of freezing
- The corridors that were navigation-optimised (Araqiel designed them for rapid movement) are now acoustically hostile. Ghost ward fragments create spatial confusion — corridors that seemed straight on the way in now feel curved. Distances that took minutes to traverse feel longer. The team has to trust Brennan's pace count and Mo Chra's degraded navigation over their own spatial perception
- They reach the outer bunker. The nursery. The storage chambers. The communication logs. All of it saturated with the residual acoustic disturbance from the playback. The stone is *restless*. It has been silent for 5,000 years and now it has spoken and it does not want to be silent again

### BEAT 4: "THE REDIRECT" — Dialling Antarctica
**Setting:** Gate threshold — Mars side. The standing wave still holding.
**POV:** Cian, Brennan

**The Gate:**
- They reach the Cydonian metamaterial gateway in the canyon wall. The standing wave is still active — visible as a subtle distortion in the air within the aperture, the temperature differential between Mars atmosphere (cold, thin, unbreathable on the external side of the bunker) and the gate's transit field
- The standing wave is weakening. Brennan's instruments show harmonic degradation — the micro-fractures in the Greenland gate (5,000 years of Arctic thermal cycling) are propagating under the sustained acoustic load. The gate structure is ageing in real-time
- Time remaining: approximately 30 minutes

**The Redirect:**
- The gate is currently set to Greenland (origin point). To transit to Antarctica (Gadreel's domain / Dudael), Mo Chra must produce a new acoustic sequence — redirecting the standing wave from the Greenland stele's beacon frequency to Gadreel's Antarctic stele frequency
- This is Use 2 of 3-4. After this redirect and transit, the gate may have 1-2 uses remaining. Or it may have none. The micro-fracture propagation makes Brennan's estimate unreliable
- Mo Chra produces the redirect frequency. She is not at full capacity — the annihilation playback depleted her acoustic reserves further. The frequency is rougher than the activation sequence in Ch8. Less precise. The gate responds, but sluggishly. The standing wave flickers, re-establishes, flickers again
- Then it locks. The aperture changes. The temperature differential shifts — the air beyond the threshold is no longer the pressurised warmth of the Araqiel bunker. It is cold. Antarctic cold. Sub-ice cold. The air of a place that has been sealed beneath the Ross Ice Shelf for millennia
- Brennan's instruments: atmospheric match to projected Dudael sub-ice conditions. Breathable. Cold. The acoustic signature through the threshold is different from anything they have encountered — denser, more complex, carrying harmonics that Mo Chra identifies as *imprisonment frequencies*. The sound of Raphael's binding work. The sound of Dudael

**The Final Decision:**
- The team stands at the threshold. Behind them: the ruins of the Araqiel bunker, the acoustic memory of divine judgment, a dead world. Ahead: the prison of the False Prophet, Naamah's drill approaching from above, and the possibility of failure
- This transit is one-way. The gate may not survive another use. Even if it does, there is no one on the Antarctic side to maintain a standing wave. They go through, the gate collapses, and they are inside Dudael with no extraction
- Cian does not hesitate. Hesitation is for men who have options

> "Through."

- The team transits. Mars disappears. The cold hits them like a wall
- Behind them, on a dead world in a canyon called the Labyrinth of Night, the gate's standing wave flickers one last time and goes dark. The Cydonian metamaterial, stressed beyond its tolerances, develops new fracture lines. Brennan's estimate was optimistic. The gate has one use left. Maybe

**Chapter Close:**
- Dudael. Sub-ice. The acoustic environment is unlike anything they have experienced — every surface hums with binding frequencies, the architecture of imprisonment, Raphael's work echoing through corridors that were built for war and remade for containment
- Somewhere above them, the faint acoustic vibration of Naamah's drill. She is close. She has been drilling for seven years. She is very close
- Mo Chra hums. Weak. Depleted. But present. The sword recognises this place. She was here once, 5,000 years ago, when Liaigh built it. She remembers
- Liaigh speaks:

> "WELCOME TO THE PRISON I BUILT. MAY GOD FORGIVE ME FOR WHAT I MUST NOW WATCH THEE FAIL TO PREVENT."

- **Chapter ends. The Dudael sequence begins.**

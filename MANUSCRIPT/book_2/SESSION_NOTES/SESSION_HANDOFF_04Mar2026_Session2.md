## 🔴 TIMELINE CONFLICT — REQUIRES AUTHOR DECISION

**The problem:** The system prompt and session handoffs carry the anchor:
"Book 1 Epilogue: October 2026 — Titan-1 launches." But the published
Book 1 manuscript has Brennan say: "2026 opposition — October 15th from
Boca Chica. Hohmann transfer trajectory: seven months transit. Arrived
May 2027. All five units operational in situ now."

The rover launch is EARLY in Book 1 (Chapter 13 briefing). The anti-singularity
happens AFTER the rovers are operational on Mars (post-May 2027). The stele
discovery, anti-singularity, Chapter 15 dissolution — all occur with rovers
already on Mars for months.

**This means:**
- Book 1's main action extends well into 2027, not ending October 2026
- "Seven weeks after anti-singularity" places Book 2 Chapter 1 in
  **mid-to-late 2027**, not early 2026

**Options:**
1. **Late 2027** — if the anti-singularity happens post-May 2027 (published
   manuscript suggests this)
2. **October 2026 + reconciliation** — if the system prompt anchors are correct
   and there's a timeline document Agent 1 hasn't seen
3. **Something else** — check offline planning documents

**Current draft uses:** Relative timestamp only ("seven weeks"). No month/year
committed. Chapter can be saved to MANUSCRIPT once date is confirmed.

**Action required:** Chris to verify against offline planning documents.

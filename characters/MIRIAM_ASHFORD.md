## THE BACKSTORY

### The Mother: Victoria Ashford

**Victoria Ashford** was a rising star in international finance. Cambridge-educated, brilliant, ruthlessly competent. By her early 30s, she held senior positions in institutions that moved billions.

She believed she worked for legitimate banking interests. Complex, yes. Ethically grey, perhaps. But legal. Respectable.

She had no idea she was working for the **Harlot**.

**The Compartmentalization:**

The Synagogue of Satan operates through layers of legitimacy. The outer layers — the ones Victoria inhabited — handle real finance, real investments, real wealth management. They provide cover, generate profits, and maintain the respectable facade.

Victoria never saw what lay beneath. Until they demanded her daughter.

### The Demand

Miriam was seven years old when it started.

"Requests" for the child to attend certain gatherings. Invitations to exclusive retreats. Pressure — subtle at first, then less so — for Victoria to offer her daughter to the inner circle.

**What Victoria Discovered:**

When she began investigating why powerful people wanted access to her daughter, she uncovered:

- Pedophile networks at the highest levels of finance and government
- Satanic Ritual Abuse ceremonies disguised as "exclusive events"
- The true nature of the organization she served
- Names, dates, accounts, evidence

The Harlot's empire was built on more than money. It was built on the systematic corruption and destruction of innocence. Children were currency. Souls were the product.

### The Mother's Choice

Victoria had a choice:

1. Comply and rise higher in the organization
2. Refuse and be destroyed

She chose a third option: **fight**.

**The Files:**

Over eighteen months, Victoria secretly compiled everything:

- Account numbers linking to human trafficking operations
- Names of participants in ritual abuse — politicians, executives, royalty
- Evidence of blackmail operations using compromised children
- The organizational structure of the Synagogue of Satan's financial arm
- Bloodline connections to Nephilim families

She created multiple backups. She prepared escape routes. She trained her daughter to survive.

Then they came for her.

### The Night Everything Ended

Miriam was nine years old.

> *"They came at 3 AM. Mother woke me, pressed a flash drive into my hand, told me to run to Uncle James — not really an uncle, a trusted ally. She said she loved me. She said she was sorry. She said to never stop fighting.*
>
> *I heard the gunshots as I climbed out the window. Three of them. Then silence.*
>
> *I never saw her body. They never found it. Officially, Victoria Ashford disappeared — embezzlement scandal, fled the country. I know better."*

### Twenty Years of Running

From age 9 to 29, Miriam has been hunted.

**The Early Years (Age 9-18):**

- Raised by her mother's allies — people who knew the truth
- Moved constantly; never stayed anywhere longer than a year
- Homeschooled in multiple countries
- The files hidden, copied, distributed to trusted parties as insurance
- Multiple assassination attempts; she survived them all

**The Training:**

Her protectors taught her to survive:

| Skill | Proficiency |
|-------|-------------|
| **Self-defense** | Krav Maga, Filipino martial arts; fights dirty, fights to kill |
| **Firearms** | Competent with handguns; prefers knives (quieter) |
| **Tradecraft** | Counter-surveillance, false identities, secure communication |
| **Financial analysis** | Her mother's gift; can read a money trail like a map |
| **Languages** | English, Hebrew, French, German, passable Arabic |

**Martial Prowess — Calibrated:**

Miriam is dangerous. She has survived twenty years of being hunted. She has killed men who tried to take her.

But she is not Cian's equal in combat. Not even close.

Cian has 2,600 years of martial experience. He has fought in every type of warfare humans have invented. He has faced giants, Nephilim, demons, and armies. He is, quite possibly, the most skilled mortal combatant who has ever lived.

Miriam is skilled enough to survive. Skilled enough to fight beside him. Skilled enough that he respects her competence and doesn't try to shelter her.

But when they fight together, she handles two enemies while he handles twenty.

> **Miriam:** *[after their first real fight together]* "You killed nine of them. In the time it took me to kill two."
>
> **Cian:** "I've had practice."
>
> **Miriam:** "How much practice?"
>
> **Cian:** "...Twenty-six centuries of practice."
>
> **Miriam:** "That's cheating."
>
> **Cian:** "It's surviving."

She knows her role. She watches his back, handles threats he can't reach in time, and trusts him to do the heavy killing. They complement each other — she is precision where he is overwhelming force.

**The Adult Years (Age 18-29):**

- Attended university under a false identity (finance, naturally)
- Worked in legitimate financial analysis while hunting the hunters
- Built her own network of survivors and allies
- The files became her weapon AND her shield — "If I die, everything goes public"
- Began actively investigating the Synagogue of Satan

### The Tobit Pattern

What neither Cian nor Miriam realizes: Raphael is doing EXACTLY what he did for Tobias — because he is the SAME ANGEL.

In the Book of Tobit, the Archangel Raphael guided young Tobias to Sarah — a woman whose seven previous husbands had been killed by the demon Asmodeus on their wedding nights. Raphael arranged the marriage and vanquished the demon.

**Miriam's Pattern:**

- Her mother died protecting her
- Her protectors over the years have died or disappeared
- Men who tried to help her have met bad ends
- And yet *she* survives

There is something hunting her. Something demonic, assigned to destroy her since the SRA ceremonies of her childhood. It cannot touch her directly — perhaps because of her mother's prayers, perhaps because of her own faith — but it destroys those around her.

The angel knows. Raphael has been waiting for Cian to find her.

Because Raphael intends to destroy the thing hunting her. Just as he destroyed Asmodeus.

> **Raphael:** *[to Cian]* "UPON THE HOUR OF THY COVENANTAL COMMITMENT TO THIS WOMAN, THE CREATURE THAT HATH PURSUED HER SINCE THE DARK CEREMONIES OF HER CHILDHOOD SHALL MANIFEST IN ITS FULLNESS. IT SHALL ENDEAVOR TO SUNDER THE UNION AS IT HATH SUNDERED ALL PRIOR ATTEMPTS TO PROTECT HER."
>
> **Cian:** "And then?"
>
> **Raphael:** "AND THEN I SHALL INSTRUCT THIS ABOMINATION IN THE NATURE OF CELESTIAL JUSTICE. I CONSIGNED ASMODEUS TO THE UTTERMOST PARTS OF EGYPT WHEN HE THREATENED THE UNION OF TOBIAS AND SARAH. THIS CREATURE SHALL FARE NO BETTER."

The fifth marriage will not end like the fourth. This time, the Healing of God is ready.

## THE JEWISH ELEMENT

### Heritage and Faith

Miriam is a **daughter of Israel** — the angel's words, not hers.

Her family descended from Sephardic Jews who fled Spain during the Inquisition, eventually settling in England. Her mother, Victoria, was secular — focused on career and success — but her grandmother kept the faith alive.

### The Shared Calendar

One of the first things Miriam notices about Cian is that he keeps the same days she does.

He rests on Sabbath. He observes the festivals. He avoids certain hours for operations. When she asks why, he explains about the heavenly liturgy — about Raphael's obligations to the eternal worship before the Throne.

> **Miriam:** "It's almost three. He'll be at Mincha."
>
> **Cian:** *[staring]* "...How do you—"
>
> **Miriam:** "I'm Jewish, Cian. I know when afternoon prayers are. If angels participate in the heavenly liturgy, they'd follow the same schedule."
>
> **Cian:** "Twenty-six centuries with him, and I never had anyone to explain it to who just... understood."
>
> **Miriam:** "That must have been lonely."
>
> **Cian:** "You have no idea."

This shared rhythm becomes a foundation of their relationship. She doesn't merely tolerate his strangeness — she *inhabits the same calendar*. The Sabbath, the festivals, the daily pauses for prayer — these are not foreign to her. They are home.

> **Cian:** "Every woman I've ever loved, I had to explain this. The Sabbath. The feasts. Why I wouldn't fight on certain days. They accepted it, but they didn't *understand* it."
>
> **Miriam:** "My grandmother lit candles every Friday. I know what Shabbat means."
>
> **Cian:** *[quietly]* "I haven't had a Shabbat with someone who understood in... centuries."
>
> **Miriam:** "Then we'll keep it together."

**The Grandmother:**

> *"My grandmother lit Shabbat candles every Friday. She kept kosher. She made me memorize prayers I didn't understand. When I asked why, she said: 'Because one day you will need them. When the darkness comes, you will need something to hold onto.'"*
>
> *"I thought she was superstitious. Old-fashioned. Then the darkness came. And I held onto those prayers like a drowning woman holds a rope."*

**Faith Through Trauma:**

During the SRA, the cultists tried to make her renounce her heritage. They forced her to desecrate the Sabbath. They made her eat unclean things. They tried to break her connection to the God of her ancestors.

It didn't work.

Something in Miriam — perhaps her grandmother's prayers, perhaps her own stubborn soul — refused to break. She recited the Shema in her head during the rituals. She held onto her identity when everything else was stripped away.

> *"They wanted me to curse God. I wouldn't. I don't know why — I was nine, I barely understood what I was refusing — but I wouldn't. They hurt me for it. But I wouldn't."*

**The Complicated Faith:**

Miriam's faith is not simple or clean. She is angry at God. She has screamed at the sky more times than she can count. She has demanded answers that never came.

But she has never stopped believing.

When she sees Cian's angel manifest — when she witnesses the divine made real — something in her breaks open. The faith she held through the darkness is *vindicated*.

> **Miriam:** *(after seeing the angel for the first time)* "It's real. It's ALL real. The prayers... the candles... my grandmother..."
>
> **Cian:** "Yes."
>
> **Miriam:** *(weeping)* "Then why? Why did they let it happen? Why did my mother die? Why did I—"
>
> **Cian:** "I've been asking that question for twenty-six centuries. I don't have an answer. But I know this: you survived. Against all odds, against all darkness, you survived. That's not nothing."
>
> **Miriam:** "Is that enough?"
>
> **Cian:** "It has to be. For now."

### The Secret She Keeps

Miriam now carries knowledge that Cian has been seeking for 2,000 years. She knows the identity of his angel. She knows she is living out the Tobit pattern — the same Archangel, the same role, millennia later.

And she will never tell him.

Not to deceive him. But to protect him. To preserve the relationship that has sustained him for twenty-six centuries.

This is her gift to him: the secret she keeps.

### The Wedding

When Cian and Miriam marry, it will be a Jewish ceremony. Cian will stand under the chuppah. He will stomp on the glass.

For a man who has watched Israel scattered and persecuted for millennia, to marry a daughter of Israel — to affirm the covenant in the ancient way — will be profoundly meaningful.

> **Cian:** *(under the chuppah, looking at Miriam)* "I've waited a very long time for this."
>
> **Miriam:** "Eight hundred years?"
>
> **Cian:** "Twenty-six centuries. I just didn't know I was waiting."

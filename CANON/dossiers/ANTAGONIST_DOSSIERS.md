Vârcolac had spent three thousand years perfecting binding wards. He was summoned to Ireland.

**His Price:** A virgin maiden's blood — the sacrifice would power the wards.

In dark magic, a promised sacrifice not delivered creates a **debt**. Vârcolac was promised virgin blood of a specific lineage. That blood was taken from him.

The debt compounds. Interest accrues. Vârcolac is patient.

> **Raphael:** *"THE VAMPIRE OPERATETH BY PRINCIPLES THAT PREDATE HUMAN COMMERCE. THE BLOOD DEBT IS REAL TO HIM — AS REAL AS ANY MORTAL CONTRACT. HE BELIEVETH, WITH ABSOLUTE CONVICTION, THAT HE IS OWED."*

### The Vendetta — Blood Debt, Not Apotheosis

Vârcolac is not hunting Niamh's descendants for mystical apotheosis. He is hunting them because *he is owed.*

After 2,600 years, the interest has accumulated to horrifying proportions. He calculates that eliminating the *entire bloodline* would balance the ledger.

**It is not personal hatred. It is accounting.**

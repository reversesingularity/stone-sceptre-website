# AUTHOR'S NOTE

The Dagon/mitre connection has been noted by various researchers and commentators over the years. By embedding this as subtle texture rather than explicit claim, the series:

1. Lets informed readers feel clever for catching it
2. Avoids alienating readers who might find explicit claims offensive
3. Maintains plausible deniability ("We never said THAT institution specifically")
4. Creates the kind of "iceberg worldbuilding" where 90% is below the surface
5. Makes Naamah more terrifying — her influence is everywhere but invisible

The best conspiracy in fiction is the one the reader assembles themselves from scattered pieces. You've given them the pieces. Let them build it.

*"She doesn't build. She inherits."*

That single line, in the right scene, does more work than a thousand words of explicit explanation.

The Divine Journey

When Jerusalem falls and the sacred bloodline faces extinction, an unlikely company embarks on an extraordinary voyage guided by divine providence and ancient prophecy.

Follow Baruch ben Neriah as he chronicles the epic journey of the prophet Jeremiah, the royal daughters Tea Tephi and Scota, and the warrior-prince Gathelus across treacherous seas and supernatural perils. From the burning ruins of the Holy City to the mystical shores of the Celtic lands, witness the transplantation of the Davidic line and the fulfillment of prophecies that will shape the destiny of nations.

This is a tale of faith tested by fire, love blooming amid chaos, and divine purposes unfolding through the courage of mortal hearts. Experience battles against supernatural enemies, the discovery of lost tribes, and the establishment of kingdoms that will endure through the ages.

The Author

Kerman Gild brings together extensive research into ancient history, Celtic mythology, and Hebrew tradition in this extraordinary epic. Drawing from the chronicles of Baruch ben Neriah and weaving together historical possibility with legendary grandeur, Gild combines scholarly attention to period detail with the storytelling power of classical epic literature.

Years of study into the migration patterns of ancient peoples, the genealogies of royal houses, and the spiritual significance of archaeological discoveries inform every page of this remarkable chronicle. From the burning ruins of Jerusalem to the mystical shores of Ireland, The Stone and the Sceptre: A Scribe's Tale represents a unique fusion of historical research and epic storytelling.

About This Work

This novel presents a fictional account based on historical traditions, Celtic legends, and biblical narratives. While grounded in extensive research, it remains a work of imaginative literature that explores the themes of divine providence, royal lineage, and the establishment of kingdoms through the lens of epic storytelling.

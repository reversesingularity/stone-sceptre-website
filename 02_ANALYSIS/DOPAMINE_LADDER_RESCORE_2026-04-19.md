## What the Edits Fixed

### 1. Ch9 — Mo Chrá's Acoustic Response to Grace (+1.35 Δ)
**The biggest single improvement in the book.** The prior version identified the Dismas scene's acoustic texture as the critical gap — Mo Chrá encountered a saint and produced an unmapped frequency with insufficient explanatory depth. The expansion delivers an architecturally precise taxonomy: *accumulated holiness vs. unearned frequency*, the four-tier sword-response hierarchy (prostration → reverence → sharpening → **adoration**), and the definitive line: *"A ruined biography carrying a perfect frequency."* The sword's cessation of classification is theologically precise — grace does not fit the Register's categories because it operates outside the merit system the Register tracks. S3 weakness is **fully closed**. This chapter is now the book's single best chapter, operating at MASTERWORK level individually.

### 2. Epilogue — Abstract Threat → Engineered Horror (+0.80 Δ)
The prior version stated the threat; the expansion *architects* it. The 7-layer schematic is the breakthrough: Layer 6 (Band Three scaled to prophetic authority — *conditioning* the commission rather than overpowering it) is the most sophisticated piece of theological-acoustic engineering in the series. The "calibration protocol incomplete until prophetic commission is active" creates the book's most productive irony — the Witnesses' testimony **is** Azazel's laboratory. The 1,260-day weapon timeline aligned with the ministry duration is chilling precisely because the math is correct and can't be argued with. N3 (setup/payoff), N6 (series threading), and S3 all improve substantially.

### 3. Ch2 — World Tasting Wrong (+0.55 Δ)
The "first threads" expansion — *"The acoustic equivalent of a stain spreading through clean water — invisible at the source, imperceptible at the edges, but changing the composition of the medium it travelled through with every molecule it touched"* — closes the Part 1 sensory-immersion gap with precision. Mo Chrá's 5.8 Hz descent below Schumann baseline as fear rather than combat-mode is an excellent acoustic characterisation. The 432 Hz resolution moment (creation frequency completing the Watcher frequency it lacks) is the chapter's conceptual payoff and correctly invokes the Oiketerion Principle without stating it explicitly.

### 4. Ch15 — Miriam Wrist Interiority (+0.50 Δ)
The deliberate exposure vs. habitual concealment binary is character-defining. *"The baptism had not erased the mark. The baptism had changed what the mark **meant**."* The cognitive fossil entry update — from *habitual concealment, post-threshold. Monitor* to *deliberate exposure, post-threshold. Acknowledged* — uses the warrior's operational vocabulary as a vehicle for tenderness. This is formally elegant. C4 (character arc micro-progress) is now fully realised for Miriam at this scene's location; the splinter payoff at the biometric gates integrates cleanly with the Ch13 plant.

### 5. Ch13 — Splinter Resonance Seed + Time Dilation Update (+0.40 Δ)
Both expansions integrate without tonal disruption. The Tamiel-calibrated splinter's passive resonance with Tamiel-derived gates is operationally logical and pays off immediately in Ch15. The ratio update (1:41.3 → 1:46.2, accelerating; Day 1,000 at 406 days) converts the Day 1,000 wall from abstract number to countdown with real urgency. N6 improves across both plants.

### 6. Ch11 — Day 1,000 Seed (+0.33 Δ)
The fourth convergence (time dilation approaching infinity at day 1,000, currently 792 days away) is correctly withheld from James — Brennan files it as belonging to a "jurisdiction his engineering could not reach." This is excellent character restraint. The plant is present but **quiet**. N6 improves. The underlying structural weaknesses of the Earth Interlude II format are not resolved by this expansion (see Remaining Gaps).

### 7. Ch5 — James/Brennan Camaraderie Beat (+0.30 Δ)
The KIA form deleted rather than completed is the expansion's best moment — Brennan arguing from the spectrograph while James deletes the paperwork is the partnership's theology in one gesture. *"Outstanding"* as the chapter's final beat remains the book's most load-bearing single word. C3 improves. The 200-word allocation is efficiently deployed.

### 8. Ch12 — Mo Chrá Band Three Silent Filing (+0.30 Δ)
The sword's silent filing of Band Three's scalability to prophetic frequencies is the Book 4 seed the chapter needed. Executed quietly — Mo Chrá's hum registering the theorem's implications while Cian moves to the next briefing block. This is the correct register (not stated, filed). N6 strengthens. The existing Phantom Banter architecture is untouched and remains the chapter's dominant strength.

## Remaining Gaps — What Prevents MASTERWORK (23.0)

### Gap 1 — Earth Interlude II (Ch11) at 20.93: STRUCTURAL, NOT RESOLVABLE BY EXPANSION
Ch11 is the book's **only chapter in COMPETENT territory** and the primary drag on the aggregate score. The S1/S2 scores (3.20/4) reflect the Earth Interlude format's built-in limitation: the server room POV produces *relayed* physicality (the ward runner reports, the deceased patient's substrate) rather than *immediate* physicality (body, breath, hands). The Day 1,000 expansion cannot fix this because the problem is architectural. What Ch11 currently lacks that Ch13 has: a moment where the engineering mind **fails** and something underneath is exposed. Ch13 has the Toulouse breach forcing that. Ch11 needs an equivalent destabilisation before it can clear 22.0. A 350–400 word insertion where Brennan holds the substrate for the first time and the Cydonian resonance does something *unexpected* to his hands — something Mo Chrá would have classified and that the engineer cannot — would lift S1/S2 and C1 simultaneously.

### Gap 2 — Ch5 S-domain at 3.25/4: PARTIALLY ADDRESSABLE
S1 (physical/visceral) and S2 (environment) remain the chapter's floor. The server room exists through its instruments, not its atmosphere. This chapter is the book's only early-book chapter that occurs in daylight (Stewart Island, spring) but the outside is effectively absent. A 150-word insertion establishing Brennan's bodily state in the aftermath — the cold, the light through the basement window, the specific nausea of two hours' adrenaline leaving a body — would lift S1 without disrupting the chapter's perfectly calibrated tone.

### Gap 3 — Ch12 at 21.95: WITHIN ONE EXPANSION OF NEAR-MASTERWORK CEILING
The chapter is one good insertion from 22.5+. The Band Three expansion adds N6, but the chapter's S-domain (3.50/4) can improve. The Phantom Banter collapses at the Mark with the right instinct — *"The voice caught. Not in his throat. In his chest"* — but the physical aftermath of that collapse (Cian's body after the comedy falls away, the specific sensation of standing in a garden briefing two prophets about the apocalypse) is described at register-level rather than sensation-level. A 200-word somatic anchor here would lift S1 and P3 simultaneously.

### Gap 4 — Series-Level S-Domain Variance
The eight edited chapters score S at: 3.70 / 3.25 / 3.75 / 3.20 / 3.50 / 3.50 / 3.70 / 3.70. The ceiling appears to be 3.75 (Ch9). The 4.00/4 score is available in principle — Ch9's Dismas confession sequence is S3 perfect — but S1 (body) and S2 (environment) are pulling the average down. The fallen-world chapters (Ch5, Ch11) are in the 3.2–3.25 range; even Ch13 at 3.50 reflects the structural cost of the Earth Interlude format. **The book cannot reach 23.0 aggregate without resolving at least two Earth Interlude S-domain scores to 3.5+.** Ch13 is already there. Ch11 is the critical remaining target.

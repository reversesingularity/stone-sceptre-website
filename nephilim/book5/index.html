The Appointed Hour

The witnesses fell in Jerusalem. The world — or what it has become in forty-two months — celebrated. For three and a half days the testimony went silent. For three and a half days, the Dragon allowed itself to believe it had finally done what the Flood failed to complete.

Then the breath came back.

What follows the resurrection of the witnesses is not a continuation of the testimony. It is the judgement that the testimony was the evidence for. Every seal was a preparation. Every trumpet a signal. Every bowl a consequence. The sequence Revelation recorded in advance has been running on schedule since Ohya rose and Azazel walked free. It does not require adjustment. It does not require help.

What it requires is witness. Presence. Faithfulness to a commission that began on a riverbank in Babylon in 586 BCE and ends — finally, completely, irreversibly — on the side of everything that was always going to win.

Cian mac Morna has carried Mo Chrá for twenty-six centuries. He has fought in every theatre of the war between heaven and the Watchers. He has never fought a war this openly contested at the cosmic level, in a timeline this compressed, against enemies this fully revealed.

He has also never once stopped. And the commission does not end early.

THE NEPHILIM CHRONICLES BOOK FIVE is the series conclusion — a five-book biblical apocalyptic thriller drawing on 1 Enoch, the Book of Jubilees, and the prophecies of Revelation. For readers who started in Cydonia and knew, even then, that this was where it was always going.

About the Author

Kerman Gild brings together extensive research in biblical prophecy, ancient Jewish apocalyptic literature, and Celtic history to craft The Nephilim Chronicles. Drawing from primary sources including the Book of 1 Enoch, the Book of Jubilees, the Dead Sea Scrolls, and detailed biblical chronology, the author weaves together theological precision and narrative compulsion.

The fifth and final volume of The Nephilim Chronicles concludes the five-book arc begun on the surface of Mars in 2026 — tracing the Watcher-era conflict through Antarctic prisons, Edenic thresholds, the streets of Jerusalem under the Two Witnesses, and the final sequence of judgements that Revelation recorded in advance. Every theological thread introduced across the series resolves here.

Kerman Gild is also the author of The Stone and the Sceptre Chronicles, a companion series tracing the Davidic covenant from Jerusalem's ruin in 586 BCE through its prophesied western home.
